Property Management Onboarding Checklist: New Owner & New Tenant

First impressions set the relationship. A smooth onboarding process builds trust and prevents problems for years.

Home → Blog → Onboarding Checklists

Onboarding is your first real test as a property manager. A chaotic onboarding — missed documents, confused tenants, surprised owners — signals that your operation isn't professional. A smooth onboarding — every step completed on time, clear communication, no surprises — builds confidence that lasts for years.

Part 1: New Owner Onboarding (30 Steps)

From signed management agreement to fully operational property. Target timeline: 7-14 days.

Day 1: Agreement & Documentation

  1. ☐ Management agreement signed (both parties)
  2. ☐ Owner information form completed (contact info, emergency contact, entity details)
  3. ☐ Owner's insurance policy obtained (verify PM company is named)
  4. ☐ W-9 form collected from owner (for 1099 reporting)
  5. ☐ Banking information collected for owner disbursements (ACH details)
  6. ☐ Communication preferences documented (email, text, call? Frequency?)
  7. ☐ Maintenance authorization threshold confirmed (e.g., $500 without approval)
  8. ☐ Reserve fund amount agreed upon and requested ($500-$1,000)

Days 2-3: Property Setup

  1. ☐ Property added to PM software (address, unit details, owner linked)
  2. ☐ Property inspection completed (photos + condition report)
  3. ☐ All keys collected (front, back, mailbox, garage, gate, pool, etc.)
  4. ☐ Key inventory logged and spare copies made
  5. ☐ Lockbox installed (for maintenance access and showings)
  6. ☐ Utility accounts documented (account numbers, current responsibility)
  7. ☐ HOA information gathered (if applicable: contact, rules, dues, portal access)
  8. ☐ Existing tenant information obtained (if occupied: lease, deposit amount, payment history)

Days 3-5: Tenant & Financial Setup

  1. ☐ Existing tenant notified of management change (introduction letter with new payment instructions)
  2. ☐ Rent payment setup in PM software (amount, due date, late fee structure)
  3. ☐ Security deposit transferred to your trust account (documented with receipt)
  4. ☐ Reserve fund deposited by owner
  5. ☐ Owner portal set up and credentials sent
  6. ☐ Tenant portal set up (if existing tenant)
  7. ☐ Existing lease uploaded to PM software
  8. ☐ Lease expiration date noted and renewal date calendared

Days 5-7: Vendor & Marketing Setup

  1. ☐ Assign preferred vendors to property (based on location/property type)
  2. ☐ If vacant: professional photos taken
  3. ☐ If vacant: listing created and syndicated to rental platforms
  4. ☐ If vacant: rental price set based on market analysis

Days 7-14: Verification & Welcome

  1. ☐ Welcome call/email to owner confirming everything is set up
  2. ☐ First month's owner statement sent (even if partial month)
💡 The welcome call matters: After everything is set up, call the owner. Walk them through their owner portal, confirm their disbursement date, explain your reporting schedule, and ask if they have any questions. This single call reduces "where's my money?" calls by 80% for the first 6 months.

Part 2: New Tenant Onboarding (20 Steps)

From approved application to move-in day. Target: 3-7 days from approval to move-in.

Pre-Move-In

  1. ☐ Approval notification sent to tenant
  2. ☐ Lease prepared with all terms, addenda, and disclosures
  3. ☐ Lease signing scheduled (in-person or electronic)
  4. ☐ Collect security deposit + first month's rent (certified funds for first payment)
  5. ☐ Verify renter's insurance policy (if required)
  6. ☐ Tenant added to PM software
  7. ☐ Tenant portal set up with login credentials
  8. ☐ Welcome packet prepared

Welcome Packet Contents

  • Emergency maintenance contact number
  • How to submit maintenance requests (portal, phone, or email)
  • Rent payment instructions (portal, auto-pay setup)
  • Move-in inspection form
  • Community rules / HOA rules (if applicable)
  • Utility setup instructions (providers, account transfer)
  • Trash/recycling schedule
  • Parking information
  • Key inventory list
  • Important local numbers (police non-emergency, fire department)

Move-In Day

  1. ☐ Move-in inspection conducted with tenant (both sign)
  2. ☐ Photos taken of all rooms (timestamped, stored digitally)
  3. ☐ All keys distributed and documented
  4. ☐ Garage opener / access fob provided
  5. ☐ Utility meters read and recorded
  6. ☐ Mailbox key provided
  7. ☐ Show tenant how to operate: thermostat, appliances, water shut-off, breaker box

Post-Move-In

  1. ☐ Auto-pay enrollment encouraged (send instructions)
  2. ☐ Tenant emergency contact collected
  3. ☐ Vehicle information collected (make, model, plate — for parking)
  4. ☐ Pet registration confirmed (if applicable: breed, weight, vaccination records)
  5. ☐ 7-day follow-up: "How's everything going? Any questions or issues?"
💡 The 7-day follow-up: One week after move-in, reach out. Ask how things are going. This catches small issues before they become complaints, and it tells the tenant "we care." It's 2 minutes that builds a relationship.

Common Onboarding Mistakes

  • Missing the security deposit transfer. When taking over from a previous manager or self-managing owner, get the security deposit transferred to your trust account with documentation. You're liable once you take over management.
  • Not reviewing the existing lease. Read the entire lease before taking over. Know the terms, renewal date, pet policies, and anything unusual.
  • Skipping the tenant introduction letter. Tenants need to know: who you are, where to pay rent, how to submit maintenance requests, and who to call in an emergency. Send this day one.
  • No welcome call to the owner. The owner just entrusted you with their most valuable asset. A 10-minute call costs nothing and builds years of loyalty.
  • Not setting expectations. Tell the owner exactly when they'll receive reports, when they'll get paid, and how you handle maintenance. No surprises.
