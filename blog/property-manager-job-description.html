What Does a Property Manager Do? (Core Duties)

A property manager is the operational backbone of a rental portfolio. They're responsible for maximizing owner returns while maintaining tenant satisfaction. Here's the breakdown:

Daily Responsibilities

  • Tenant communication: Responding to maintenance requests, lease inquiries, and complaints within 24 hours
  • Work order management: Creating, assigning, and tracking maintenance work orders from submission to completion
  • Rent collection: Monitoring payments, sending late notices, and initiating collections for delinquent accounts
  • Vendor coordination: Dispatching contractors for maintenance, repairs, and turnovers
  • Showing vacant units: Scheduling and conducting property tours for prospective tenants

Weekly Responsibilities

  • Property inspections: Drive-by or interior inspections based on schedule
  • Financial reporting: Updating owner statements, tracking expenses, reconciling accounts
  • Lease management: Processing applications, running background checks, preparing lease agreements
  • Marketing vacant units: Listing on Zillow, Apartments.com, Facebook Marketplace; taking photos

Monthly/Quarterly Responsibilities

  • Owner reporting: P&L statements, occupancy reports, maintenance summaries
  • Lease renewals: Market rent analysis, renewal negotiations, rent increase letters
  • Budget reviews: Comparing actual vs. budgeted expenses, adjusting forecasts
  • Compliance audits: Fair housing compliance, license renewals, insurance verification

Property Manager Job Description Template (General)

📋 Property Manager — [Your Company Name]

Location: [City, State] | Type: Full-time | Salary: $45,000–$65,000 + performance bonuses

About the role: We're looking for a detail-oriented Property Manager to oversee a portfolio of [X] residential units across [City/Region]. You'll be the primary point of contact for tenants and owners, responsible for maximizing occupancy, minimizing expenses, and delivering exceptional service.

Responsibilities:

  • Manage a portfolio of [50–150] residential units (single-family, multifamily, or mixed)
  • Handle tenant relations: applications, lease signing, maintenance requests, move-in/move-out inspections
  • Coordinate maintenance and capital improvement projects with approved vendors
  • Collect rent and manage delinquencies per company policy (target: 95%+ collection rate)
  • Market vacant properties across listing platforms; maintain vacancy rate below [X]%
  • Prepare monthly owner statements and financial reports
  • Ensure compliance with local, state, and federal housing regulations
  • Conduct regular property inspections (interior and exterior)

Requirements:

  • 2+ years property management experience (residential preferred)
  • Active real estate or property management license (state-specific)
  • Proficiency in PM software (AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager, or similar)
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Reliable transportation and valid driver's license
  • Ability to handle after-hours emergencies on a rotating basis

Nice to Have: CPM, RMP, or ARM designation · Experience with 100+ door portfolios · Bilingual (English/Spanish)

Benefits: Health/dental/vision insurance, PTO, mileage reimbursement, phone stipend, performance bonuses

Leasing Agent Job Description Template

📋 Leasing Agent — [Your Company Name]

Location: [City, State] | Type: Full-time | Salary: $30,000–$40,000 + leasing bonuses

About the role: We're hiring an energetic Leasing Agent to fill vacancies fast and deliver a 5-star move-in experience. You'll be the face of our properties for prospective tenants.

Responsibilities:

  • Respond to all rental inquiries within 2 hours during business hours
  • Schedule and conduct property showings (15–25 per week)
  • Process applications: collect documents, run screening (credit, criminal, eviction, income verification)
  • Prepare and execute lease agreements
  • Conduct move-in inspections with detailed photo documentation
  • Maintain accurate vacancy and showing activity reports
  • Post and update rental listings on Zillow, Apartments.com, Facebook, and Craigslist

Requirements: Customer service experience · High school diploma (some college preferred) · Basic computer skills · Reliable transportation · Weekend availability

Key Qualifications to Look For

Must-Have Skills

  1. Communication: Clear, professional writing and speaking. PMs interact with tenants, owners, and vendors daily — poor communication creates crises.
  2. Organization: Managing 100+ units means hundreds of tasks. Without systems, things fall through cracks. Look for candidates who describe their tracking systems.
  3. Financial literacy: PMs must read P&L statements, manage budgets, and explain financial performance to owners. This isn't optional.
  4. Conflict resolution: Tenant disputes, noise complaints, eviction conversations — PMs need composure under pressure.
  5. Tech proficiency: PM software (AppFolio, Buildium), basic spreadsheets, digital marketing platforms. Pen-and-paper PMs slow your whole operation.

Red Flags in PM Candidates

  • Can't explain how they handled a difficult tenant situation
  • No experience with PM software (steep learning curve if they're starting from zero)
  • Vague about their portfolio size and responsibilities at previous jobs
  • Focus on "managing properties" without mentioning financial results
  • No questions about your systems, processes, or team structure (they're not thinking about scale)

Interview Questions for Property Managers

Once you've attracted candidates with a strong job description, use these questions to separate the good from the great:

  1. "Walk me through how you'd handle a tenant who hasn't paid rent for 3 months and is claiming financial hardship."
  2. "What PM software have you used? What did you like/dislike about it?"
  3. "Describe a time you had to deliver bad news to a property owner. How did you handle it?"
  4. "How many units have you managed simultaneously? What systems did you use to stay organized?"
  5. "A tenant reports a water leak at 11 PM on a Friday. Walk me through your response."

Common Mistakes in PM Job Descriptions

  1. Being too vague: "Manage properties" tells candidates nothing. Specify portfolio size, property types, and key metrics they'll own.
  2. Unrealistic requirements: Requiring CPM + 10 years experience for a $45K role will get you zero applicants.
  3. Ignoring compensation: Job posts with salary ranges get 3x more applicants. Transparency wins.
  4. No growth path: Top PM talent wants to know where the role leads. Mention promotion timelines and skill development opportunities.
  5. Forgetting about culture: PMs work independently. Describe your communication style, meeting cadence, and management philosophy.
