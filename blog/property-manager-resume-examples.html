Resume Writing Tips for Property Managers

1. Lead with Numbers

Every bullet point should include a metric. Property management is a numbers business — your resume should reflect that:

  • ❌ "Managed rental properties" → ✅ "Managed 175-unit portfolio valued at $38M"
  • ❌ "Collected rent" → ✅ "Maintained 99.2% rent collection rate"
  • ❌ "Handled maintenance" → ✅ "Reduced emergency work orders by 41% through preventive maintenance program"
  • ❌ "Leased apartments" → ✅ "Executed 142 new leases, reducing average days-to-lease from 34 to 19"

2. Include the Right Keywords

3. Structure for Impact

  • Professional summary — 2-3 sentences with your biggest achievements and scope (units managed, portfolio value)
  • Experience — Reverse chronological, 5-7 bullets per role, most recent role gets the most detail
  • Education & certifications — Designations (CPM, ARM, CAM) are more valuable than degrees in PM
  • Skills section — Include PM software, financial tools, and compliance knowledge

4. Tailor to the Job Posting

Read the job description carefully and mirror their language:

  • If they emphasize "NOI growth," lead with your revenue optimization achievements
  • If they mention specific software (AppFolio, Yardi), make sure it's prominent in your resume
  • If they want "team leadership," highlight your management experience and team development

Common Resume Mistakes in Property Management

  1. No metrics — "Managed properties" tells a hiring manager nothing. Include occupancy rates, collection rates, portfolio size, and financial improvements
  2. Generic objective statement — Replace with a results-focused professional summary
  3. Listing duties instead of achievements — "Responsible for maintenance" vs. "Reduced maintenance costs by 22% through vendor renegotiation"
  4. Missing certifications — ARM, CPM, RMP, and CAM designations should be prominently displayed
  5. Too long — 1 page for <5 years experience, 2 pages max for experienced PMs
  6. No PM software mentioned — If you know AppFolio, Buildium, or Yardi, say so clearly

Frequently Asked Questions

What should a property manager put on their resume?

Focus on quantifiable achievements: occupancy rates, rent collection rates, portfolio size (units and dollar value), NOI improvements, cost savings, team size managed, and specific PM software proficiency. Include certifications (ARM, CPM, CAM) prominently.

How do I write a property manager resume with no experience?

Start with transferable skills from customer service, sales, or hospitality roles. Highlight any real estate coursework, Fair Housing training, or PM software certifications. Apply for leasing agent positions as your entry point — most PM companies promote from within.

Is a CPM certification worth it for my resume?

Yes — CPM holders earn 20-30% more than non-designated property managers. It's the gold standard certification in PM, recognized industry-wide. The investment ($5,000-8,000 and 12-18 months) pays for itself within the first year through salary increase.
