Our Top Pick: Buildium

For most small PM companies, Buildium offers the best balance of features, price, and scalability. Here's why:

  • No minimum unit requirement — you can start with 10 units and grow to 5,000+
  • All-in-one: Rent collection, maintenance, accounting, leasing, and owner portals included
  • Tenant screening built in — TransUnion credit, criminal, and eviction checks
  • Online rent payments via ACH ($0 fee) and credit card
  • Owner portals with real-time financial reporting
  • 1099 e-filing for vendors and owners
  • Listing syndication to Zillow, Apartments.com, and 20+ sites

Best Free Option: TenantCloud

If you're just starting out with fewer than 75 units and need to keep costs at zero, TenantCloud's free tier is legitimate:

  • Up to 75 units free
  • Online rent collection (ACH)
  • Maintenance request tracking
  • Basic accounting and reporting
  • Tenant and owner portals

The catch: The free tier lacks screening, marketing tools, and advanced reporting. You'll outgrow it around 50-75 units and need to upgrade ($15/unit/mo) or switch.

When to Switch from Spreadsheets to Software

If you're still using Excel or Google Sheets, here are the signs it's time to switch:

  1. You're managing 25+ units — manual tracking becomes error-prone
  2. You're missing follow-ups — late rent, maintenance requests falling through cracks
  3. Owners are asking for better reporting — monthly statements take hours to create
  4. You're hiring your first employee — they need a system, not your personal spreadsheet
  5. You're losing tenants because they can't pay online or submit maintenance requests digitally

Features That Actually Matter for Small Companies

Ignore the feature comparison charts with 100 checkboxes. Here's what actually impacts your daily operations:

Must-Have (Non-Negotiable)

  • Online rent collection (ACH + credit card) — eliminates 90% of rent collection headaches
  • Maintenance request portal — tenants submit requests digitally, you track and assign
  • Basic accounting — income/expense tracking, bank reconciliation, owner statements
  • Owner portal — owners can see their financial reports 24/7 without calling you

Nice-to-Have (Worth Paying Extra)

  • Tenant screening — saves $30-$50/applicant vs. third-party services
  • Listing syndication — auto-post vacancies to Zillow, Apartments.com, etc.
  • E-signatures — sign leases without printing/scanning
  • Inspections — mobile inspection with photos and reports

Overkill for Small Companies

  • Custom API access (you probably don't have a developer)
  • AI-powered everything (marketing buzzwords, minimal real value)
  • Enterprise reporting dashboards (10 units don't need business intelligence)

Migration Guide: Switching PM Software

If you're switching from one system to another, here's the process:

  1. Export all data from your current system (CSV format: tenants, owners, leases, ledgers)
  2. Set up new system during a quiet period (avoid first-of-month)
  3. Import historical data — most systems have migration support for common platforms
  4. Run both systems in parallel for 30 days to catch discrepancies
  5. Train your team on the new system before cutting over
  6. Notify tenants and owners about new portal access with clear instructions
