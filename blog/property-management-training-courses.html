Detailed Course Reviews

IREM Certified Property Manager (CPM)

Price: $8,000-12,000 total | Duration: 12-18 months | Format: 7 required courses + electives

The CPM is the MBA of property management. It's recognized by institutional owners, REITs, and large management firms as the top credential. The curriculum covers asset management, financial analysis, leadership, and risk management at a level that no other PM certification matches.

The ROI case: CPM-designated managers earn an average of $20,000-30,000 more per year than non-designated counterparts. If you're on a career track at a large firm, the investment pays back within 12 months.

Who should skip it: Owner-operators managing their own portfolio. If you own the company, you don't need a certification to validate your competence — you need systems to scale. The time and money are better spent on operational improvements.

NARPM Designation Courses (RMP/MPM)

Price: $500-2,000 total | Duration: 3-12 months | Format: Online courses + experience requirements

NARPM's Residential Management Professional (RMP) and Master Property Manager (MPM) designations are the standard credentials for residential PM. The courses cover operations, legal compliance, and business management. The MPM additionally requires managing 100+ units and 5+ years of experience.

The real value: NARPM membership and involvement, not just the courses. The network of 6,000+ residential PMs who attend chapter meetings, share best practices, and refer clients to each other is worth more than the coursework itself.

The weakness: Courses are lecture-heavy. You'll learn what to do but won't get the templates, SOPs, and systems to actually implement it. Plan to supplement with operational tools.

DoorGrow Mastermind

Price: $500-2,000/month | Format: Group coaching + community + content

Jason Hull's DoorGrow program focuses on helping PM owners grow their door count. The curriculum covers sales, marketing, hiring, and operations. The community of PM owners is engaged and supportive.

Strengths: Directly focused on growth (not operations). The coaching model means you get personalized advice, not just generic content. Good for PMs stuck at a growth plateau.

Weaknesses: The monthly cost adds up — 12 months at $1,000/month is $12,000, comparable to the CPM. And the "proprietary systems" they teach could be replicated with publicly available resources and strong execution.

Udemy Property Management Courses

Price: $15-50 (frequently on sale for $10-15) | Format: Video on demand

The best-selling PM courses on Udemy include Symon He's "Property Management: Managing Rental Properties" (20,000+ students) and Alex Genadinik's "Property Management Startup" (5,000+ students). Quality ranges from excellent introductions to thin overviews.

Best for: Absolute beginners who want to test whether PM is the right career for $15-50 of risk. Not sufficient for practicing PMs.

The catch: Udemy certificates carry zero professional weight. Don't put "Udemy Certified Property Manager" on your resume — it won't help.

The Hidden Cost of Training Courses

Course price is the least important cost. The real costs are:

  • Time away from operations — A 40-hour course is a full work week. What's the opportunity cost of a PM owner being in class instead of acquiring new doors?
  • Implementation gap — Most courses teach concepts without providing implementation tools. You'll need to build your own SOPs, templates, and processes after the course ends. Budget 2-4 weeks for this.
  • Ongoing requirements — CPM requires continuing education. NARPM designations require membership and renewal. Monthly coaching programs add up over time.

The most cost-effective training path: free resources for foundations → one targeted paid program for your specific gap → ongoing self-education through industry content.

After reviewing every major program, the standouts share these traits:
