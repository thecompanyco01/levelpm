The 3 ROI Metrics Every PM Should Know

1. Cash-on-Cash Return (CoC)

The most practical metric for leveraged investors. It measures annual cash flow as a percentage of actual cash invested.

CoC Return = (Annual Cash Flow ÷ Total Cash Invested) × 100

Where:
Annual Cash Flow = NOI - Annual Debt Service
Total Cash Invested = Down Payment + Closing Costs + Rehab

Good benchmark: 8-12% CoC return is considered strong. Below 5% is marginal. Above 15% is exceptional (verify the numbers — it might be too good to be true).

2. Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate)

Measures property performance independent of financing. Useful for comparing properties on an apples-to-apples basis.

Cap Rate = (Net Operating Income ÷ Purchase Price) × 100

Where:
NOI = Gross Rental Income - Operating Expenses (no mortgage)

Good benchmark: 5-8% for most residential markets. Higher cap rates exist in lower-cost markets but often come with more management headaches. Lower cap rates (3-5%) are common in premium markets.

3. Total Return on Investment

The full picture including appreciation, principal paydown, and tax benefits.

Total ROI = (Cash Flow + Appreciation + Principal Paydown + Tax Benefits) ÷ Total Investment × 100

Total ROI is harder to calculate precisely because appreciation and tax benefits vary, but it's typically 2-3x higher than cash-on-cash return alone.

How Property Managers Add ROI Value

As a PM, here's how you justify your 8-12% fee and actually increase owner ROI:

  1. Lower vacancy rates — Professional marketing and tenant retention reduce vacancy from 8%+ to under 5%
  2. Better tenant screening — Fewer evictions (each eviction costs $5,000-$10,000+ in lost rent, legal fees, and turnover)
  3. Maintenance cost control — Vendor relationships and volume discounts save 15-25% on repairs
  4. Market-rate rents — Annual rent analysis ensures properties aren't under-market (common with self-managing owners)
  5. Legal compliance — Avoiding fair housing violations and following eviction procedures properly prevents costly lawsuits
  6. Time savings — Owners recover 10-20 hours/month per property to focus on acquisition and strategy

Red Flags in Rental Property Analysis

  • 🚩 Negative cash flow — Even with appreciation hopes, negative cash flow is risky
  • 🚩 Cap rate below 3% — You're paying a premium for the location, not the income
  • 🚩 CapEx needs exceeding 20% of purchase price — You're buying a renovation project, not a rental
  • 🚩 Property taxes exceeding 2% of property value annually — Common in some markets, but it crushes returns
  • 🚩 "Pro forma" numbers vs. actuals — Always use actual rent, actual expenses, actual vacancy. Never trust seller pro formas.
