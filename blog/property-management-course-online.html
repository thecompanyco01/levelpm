Best Free Property Management Courses Online

1. Buildium Academy

Buildium's free course library covers the basics of residential property management. It's obviously designed to funnel you into using Buildium's software, but the content is genuinely useful. Covers: leasing, maintenance management, financial reporting, and Fair Housing.

Pros: Free, well-produced videos, practical focus
Cons: Software-specific examples, light on advanced topics
Best for: New PMs who want a quick foundation

2. YouTube PM Channels

Several property management professionals share excellent free content on YouTube:

  • Marc Cunningham (Grace Property Management) — Deep operational content from an experienced PM company owner
  • Jordan Muela (LeadSimple) — Business growth and operations for PM companies
  • Alex Osenenko (Fourandhalf) — Marketing and growth strategies for PMs

Pros: Free, real-world experience, variety of perspectives
Cons: Unstructured, no certification, quality varies
Best for: Self-directed learners who want real-world insights

3. HUD Fair Housing Courses

The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development offers free Fair Housing training. This is essential knowledge for every PM, and it's authoritative — straight from the regulatory source.

Best for: Compliance training (pair with practical PM training)

Best Paid Property Management Courses Online

1. IREM Certified Property Manager (CPM) — $5,000+

The gold standard of PM credentials. The CPM designation requires completing 7 courses, passing a comprehensive exam, meeting experience requirements, and following a code of ethics.

Courses include:

  • Ethics for Real Estate Managers
  • Financial Tools for Real Estate Management
  • Leadership and Human Resource Essentials
  • Marketing and Leasing
  • Managing the Physical Asset
  • Investment Real Estate Financing and Valuation
  • Property Management Plan (capstone)

Pros: Industry-recognized credential, comprehensive curriculum, networking through IREM chapters
Cons: Expensive ($5,000+ for all courses), time-intensive (12-18 months), more academic than practical
Best for: PMs managing commercial properties or large residential portfolios who want top-tier credentials

2. NARPM Designation Courses — $30-265 Each

NARPM offers three designations for residential PMs:

  • RMP (Residential Management Professional) — Entry-level designation for individual PMs
  • MPM (Master Property Manager) — Advanced designation for experienced PMs
  • CRMC (Certified Residential Management Company) — Company-level designation

Pros: Affordable per course, residential-specific, good networking through NARPM
Cons: Courses can feel dated, heavy on association culture
Best for: Residential PMs who want industry credentials without the CPM price tag

3. Udemy Property Management Courses — $15-85

Udemy has dozens of PM courses ranging from "Property Management 101" to niche topics like vacation rental management. Quality varies widely.

Top picks:

  • "The Complete Guide to Property Management" by James Cook — 4.5 stars, 12+ hours
  • "Rental Property Management" by Symon He — 4.4 stars, good for DIY landlords

Pros: Cheap, immediate access, lifetime access to materials
Cons: No credential, quality varies, some courses are just slideshows with narration
Best for: Budget-conscious learners, DIY landlords, supplementary learning

4. Coursera Real Estate Management Specialization — $49/mo

University-backed courses (typically from schools like Columbia or University of Michigan) that cover real estate fundamentals including property management topics.

Pros: University quality, structured curriculum, certificates of completion
Cons: More academic than practical, doesn't cover day-to-day PM operations deeply
Best for: People transitioning into PM from other careers who want academic credibility

Courses vs. Practical Training: What Actually Works

Here's what they don't tell you about PM courses: most of what makes a great property manager can't be learned in a course.

Courses are good for:

  • Legal compliance knowledge (Fair Housing, state laws)
  • Financial concepts (trust accounting, NOI calculations)
  • Industry credentials for career advancement
  • Vocabulary and frameworks for communicating with peers

Courses are NOT good for:

  • Judgment calls (should you approve this application?)
  • People skills (handling an angry tenant at 10 PM)
  • Operations (your specific workflow for lease renewals)
  • Scaling (how to go from 50 to 500 doors without chaos)

The most effective PM training combines:

  1. One course for foundational knowledge (NARPM or a quality Udemy course)
  2. SOPs and templates for day-to-day operations (this is what actually runs your business)
  3. Mentorship/shadowing for judgment and people skills
  4. Continuous learning through industry podcasts, conferences, and peer groups

Building Your PM Learning Path

If You're Starting from Zero

  1. Complete your state licensing course (if required)
  2. Watch Buildium Academy's free courses for basics
  3. Download free SOP templates and start customizing them
  4. Find a mentor or shadow at a local PM company
  5. Join NARPM for networking and continuing education

If You're an Experienced PM Wanting to Scale

  1. Get operational SOPs in place (the PM Scaling Kit covers this)
  2. Pursue NARPM designations (RMP/MPM) for credibility
  3. Study growth-focused content (DoorGrow, BiggerPockets PM podcast)
  4. Consider IREM CPM if targeting large/commercial portfolios
  5. Attend NARPM national conference annually

If You're a PM Company Owner

  1. Build your company's internal training program using SOPs
  2. Invest in your team's certifications (it reduces turnover)
  3. Create a learning budget: $500-1,000/person/year
  4. Require annual Fair Housing refresher for all staff
  5. Build a library of recorded training (showings, calls, inspections)

The Bottom Line

The best property management course online depends on where you are in your career. New PMs should start with free resources and state licensing. Experienced PMs should invest in SOPs and systems, not more courses. Company owners should build internal training programs that scale with their team.

Don't fall into the trap of perpetual learning. One good course + practical SOPs + real-world experience beats a library of certificates that never get applied.
