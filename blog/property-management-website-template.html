Property Management Website Template: What Every PM Site Needs to Convert Owners in 2026

Your property management website is your 24/7 sales rep. When a property owner Googles "property management [your city]" and lands on your site, you have about 8 seconds to convince them to stay.

Most PM websites fail at this. They look outdated, lack clear calls-to-action, and talk about features instead of outcomes. Here's the template for a PM website that actually converts visitors into clients.

Essential Pages Every PM Website Needs

1. Homepage

Your homepage must answer three questions in 5 seconds:

  1. What do you do? "Full-service property management in [City]"
  2. Why should I care? "We help owners earn more while doing less"
  3. What should I do next? "Get a Free Management Quote" (big, obvious button)

Must-have elements above the fold:

  • Clear headline with your value proposition
  • Door count or years in business ("Managing 350+ doors since 2015")
  • Google review rating ("4.8★ from 127 reviews")
  • Primary CTA button ("Get a Free Quote")
  • Phone number visible in header

2. Services Page

List your services as benefits, not features:

  • ❌ "We screen tenants" → ✅ "We place quality tenants who pay on time and stay longer"
  • ❌ "Monthly reporting" → ✅ "Know exactly how your investment is performing every month"
  • ❌ "Maintenance coordination" → ✅ "24/7 maintenance response so you never get a midnight call"

3. About Page

Property owners want to know the people behind the company:

  • Team photos (real photos, not stock images)
  • Your story — why you started this company
  • Credentials (NARPM, CPM, state licenses)
  • Company values (not corporate fluff — real principles you operate by)

4. Pricing / Fee Structure Page

Transparency wins. Most PM companies hide their pricing — being upfront builds trust:

  • Management fee: X% of monthly rent collected (typical: 8-12%)
  • Leasing fee: X% of first month's rent or flat fee
  • Any additional fees clearly listed
  • "No hidden fees" guarantee if applicable

5. Reviews / Testimonials Page

Dedicate an entire page to social proof:

  • Embed your Google reviews directly
  • Feature 5-10 detailed testimonials from property owners
  • Include specifics: "They helped me go from 5 to 35 doors in 18 months"
  • Video testimonials are 10x more powerful than text

6. Blog

Your blog drives organic traffic from Google. Target keywords like:

  • "How much do property managers charge in [city]?"
  • "[City] rental market trends 2026"
  • "Property management vs self-managing rental property"
  • "How to find a good property manager in [city]"

Each post should end with a CTA: "Thinking about professional management? Get a free quote."

7. Contact Page

  • Contact form (name, email, phone, property address, number of units)
  • Phone number (prominently displayed)
  • Email address
  • Office address with Google Maps embed
  • Office hours

Conversion Optimization Tips

  1. Add a "Free Rental Analysis" offer. Property owners love free valuations. Collect their info and follow up.
  2. Use exit-intent popups. When someone's about to leave, offer a free guide or market report.
  3. Add live chat. Even if it's just you responding from your phone, live chat converts 3-5x better than contact forms.
  4. Install tracking. Google Analytics and heatmaps (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity) show you where visitors drop off.
  5. A/B test your headline. Small changes to your homepage headline can increase conversions by 20-50%.

Tools to Build Your PM Website

  • WordPress + Jesuspended theme: Most popular option, highly customizable
  • Squarespace: Beautiful templates, easy for non-technical users
  • Webflow: Most design flexibility, moderate learning curve
  • Carrd: Dead simple single-page sites for new PM companies

Your website doesn't need to be fancy — it needs to be clear. Clear value proposition, clear social proof, clear call-to-action. Get those three right and your website will be your best salesperson.
