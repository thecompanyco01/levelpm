Why Property Managers Need Virtual Assistants

If you're managing 50+ doors and still answering every phone call, processing every maintenance request, and chasing down every rent payment yourself, you're not running a business — you're working a job. A virtual assistant is the fastest, most cost-effective way to buy back your time.

The math is simple: a full-time in-office admin costs $35,000-45,000/year plus benefits. A trained VA from the Philippines costs $10,000-16,000/year. That's the same output at 30-40% of the cost.

What Tasks to Delegate to a PM Virtual Assistant

🏠 Tenant Communication (High Volume, Easy to Delegate)

  • Answer and return phone calls (using your VoIP system)
  • Respond to tenant emails and portal messages
  • Send rent reminders and late notices
  • Schedule property showings and coordinate with agents
  • Process maintenance requests (triage and dispatch)
  • Follow up on work orders with vendors
  • Handle lease renewal notices and paperwork

📊 Administrative Tasks

  • Data entry into PM software (AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager)
  • Process rental applications and run background checks
  • Prepare monthly owner statements and reports
  • Update property listings on Zillow, Apartments.com, etc.
  • Manage vendor invoices and payment processing
  • File and organize digital documents
  • Calendar management and appointment scheduling

📱 Marketing & Listings

  • Post and update rental listings across platforms
  • Respond to listing inquiries
  • Create and schedule social media posts
  • Manage Google Business Profile reviews
  • Send email newsletters to owner prospects
  • Update website content and blog posts

💰 Financial Tasks

  • Reconcile bank statements
  • Process security deposit dispositions
  • Track income and expenses by property
  • Generate financial reports for owners
  • Follow up on outstanding invoices
💡 Rule of thumb: If a task doesn't require being physically present at a property or making a high-stakes business decision, a trained VA can handle it. Start by tracking your time for one week — you'll be shocked how much of your day is delegatable admin work.

Hiring Process: Step by Step

  1. Define the role clearly — Write a detailed job description listing specific tasks, tools they'll use (AppFolio, Google Workspace, etc.), and required hours/timezone overlap
  2. Post on 2-3 platforms — Cast a wide net. OnlineJobs.ph + Upwork is a good combo
  3. Screen with a skills test — Send 5-10 candidates a practical test: write a tenant email, process a mock maintenance request, enter data into a spreadsheet
  4. Video interview top 3 — Assess communication skills, English proficiency, internet connection quality, and cultural fit
  5. Start with a 2-week paid trial — Give real (supervised) tasks. Evaluate responsiveness, accuracy, and initiative
  6. Hire the best performer — If none pass trial, start over. Don't settle.

Training Your VA: The 30-Day Plan

Week 1: Foundation

  • Company overview: your values, service standards, and owner expectations
  • PM software training (screen-share sessions, recorded for reference)
  • Communication standards: email templates, phone scripts, response times
  • Shadow you on every task they'll eventually own

Week 2: Supervised Practice

  • VA handles tasks while you review before sending/publishing
  • Daily check-in calls (15-30 min) to address questions
  • Document every process as an SOP (the VA writes it, you review)
  • Introduce vendor and tenant communication under supervision

Week 3: Gradual Independence

  • VA handles routine tasks independently; you spot-check
  • Move to every-other-day check-ins
  • Add more complex tasks as confidence grows
  • Start tracking KPIs: response time, accuracy, tasks completed

Week 4: Full Autonomy on Core Tasks

  • VA owns all delegated tasks with weekly reviews
  • Establish escalation protocol: what requires your input vs. what they decide
  • SOP library should be 80% complete
  • Formal 30-day review: keep, adjust, or replace
💡 Critical: The #1 reason PMs fail with VAs is lack of SOPs. If you can't document a process step-by-step, you can't delegate it. Write SOPs BEFORE you hire. Our free SOP templates give you a head start.

Tools Your VA Needs

  • PM Software: AppFolio, Buildium, or Rent Manager (VA login with appropriate permissions)
  • Communication: Google Voice or VoIP phone system (so they call from your business number)
  • Project Management: Asana, Monday.com, or Trello for task tracking
  • Time Tracking: Time Doctor, Hubstaff, or Toggl
  • Video: Zoom or Google Meet for check-ins
  • Password Manager: LastPass or 1Password (never share passwords directly)
  • Screen Recording: Loom for async training and updates

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Hiring too fast — Take time to find the right person. A bad VA wastes more time than no VA.
  2. Not creating SOPs first — You're setting them up to fail without documented processes.
  3. Micromanaging — The whole point is freeing your time. Trust the process after training.
  4. Giving too much access too soon — Start with limited permissions, expand as trust is built.
  5. No time tracking — If you're paying hourly, verify hours. Use automated tracking software.
  6. Ignoring timezone issues — Ensure at least 4 hours of overlap with your business hours for real-time communication.
