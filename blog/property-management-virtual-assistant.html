Property Management Virtual Assistant: The Complete Guide to Scaling With VAs (2026)

Updated March 2026 · 12 min read · By LevelPM

Here's a dirty secret of property management: the companies scaling fastest aren't hiring more local staff — they're hiring virtual assistants. A skilled PM virtual assistant costs $5-12/hour, handles 80% of your admin work, and lets you add 50-100 doors without adding a single in-office employee.

This guide covers everything: what tasks to delegate, where to find the best VAs, how much to pay, how to train them, and the exact SOPs that make the relationship work. Whether you're managing 30 doors or 300, a VA can be the highest-ROI hire you ever make.

Why Property Management VAs Are a Game-Changer

The typical property management company spends 60-70% of revenue on payroll. When you're managing 50-150 doors, that margin is razor-thin. VAs fundamentally change the equation:

  • Cost savings: A full-time US admin assistant costs $35,000-45,000/year. A full-time VA from the Philippines costs $10,000-18,000/year for equal or better work quality in admin tasks.
  • Scalability: Add a VA in 1-2 weeks vs. 6-8 weeks for local hiring (job posting, interviews, onboarding).
  • Coverage: Time zone differences mean your business handles requests 16+ hours/day instead of 8.
  • Focus: You stop doing $15/hour tasks and focus on $150/hour activities — owner acquisition, strategy, relationship building.
📊 Real numbers: A PM company managing 120 doors hired their first VA at $8/hour full-time ($1,280/month). Within 3 months, they'd added 40 new doors because the owner finally had time to sell. ROI: $4,000/month in new management fees from a $1,280 investment.

What Tasks to Delegate to a PM Virtual Assistant

Tier 1: Start Here (Week 1)

  • Tenant communication: Responding to maintenance requests, lease questions, general inquiries
  • Listing management: Posting and updating rental listings on Zillow, Apartments.com, Facebook Marketplace
  • Data entry: Updating property management software (AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager)
  • Phone screening: Initial tenant screening calls, scheduling showings
  • Document preparation: Lease packets, move-in/move-out documents, notices

Tier 2: After 30 Days

  • Maintenance coordination: Dispatching vendors, following up on work orders, getting quotes
  • Rent collection follow-up: Late payment calls, payment plan coordination
  • Owner reporting: Preparing monthly owner statements, compiling financial reports
  • Tenant screening: Running background checks, verifying employment, checking references
  • Social media: Managing Facebook, Instagram, Google Business Profile

Tier 3: Experienced VA (90+ Days)

  • Bookkeeping: Accounts payable/receivable, bank reconciliation, invoice processing
  • Lease renewals: Managing the entire renewal process, market rent analysis
  • Vendor management: Negotiating rates, managing insurance certificates, compliance tracking
  • Marketing: Email campaigns, blog content, lead nurturing
  • CRM management: Following up with owner leads, tracking pipeline

How to Train a PM Virtual Assistant (The SOP Framework)

The #1 reason PM companies fail with VAs isn't the VA — it's the lack of standard operating procedures. If your processes live in your head, no VA (or any employee) can replicate them.

Step 1: Document Before You Delegate

Before handing off any task, create a simple SOP with:

  1. Purpose: Why this task matters (context helps VAs make better judgment calls)
  2. Steps: Numbered, sequential instructions with screenshots
  3. Tools: What software/logins they'll need
  4. Examples: 2-3 real examples of the task done correctly
  5. Escalation: When to stop and ask you instead of guessing

Step 2: The 2-Week Onboarding Plan

  • Days 1-3: Shadow you. VA watches you do each task via screen share while taking notes.
  • Days 4-7: VA does tasks while you watch. Correct in real time.
  • Days 8-10: VA works independently. You review all output at end of day.
  • Days 11-14: VA works independently. You spot-check 20% of output.

Step 3: Communication Rhythm

  • Daily: 15-minute standup (Slack or Zoom). What did you do yesterday? What are you doing today? Any blockers?
  • Weekly: 30-minute review. What went well? What needs improvement? Any SOP updates needed?
  • Monthly: Performance review + feedback. Discuss growth, raise potential, new responsibilities.

Tools for Managing Your PM Virtual Assistant

  • Communication: Slack (daily standup channel), Zoom (weekly calls), Loom (async video instructions)
  • Task management: Asana, Monday.com, or ClickUp (track what's assigned and completed)
  • Time tracking: Time Doctor or Hubstaff (activity monitoring + screenshots for accountability)
  • Screen sharing: TeamViewer or AnyDesk (for training on PM software)
  • Documentation: Google Docs/Notion for SOPs, Loom for video SOPs
  • Phone: OpenPhone or Grasshopper (US phone number for tenant/owner calls)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. No SOPs before delegating. If you can't explain the process step-by-step, your VA can't do it. Write the SOP first.
  2. Hiring too cheap. A $3/hour VA will cost you more in mistakes and re-work than a $7/hour VA who gets it right the first time.
  3. Micromanaging. The point is to free your time. Check output, not every keystroke.
  4. Not giving feedback. VAs can't improve if you don't tell them what's wrong. Be direct and specific.
  5. Treating them like a tool, not a person. VAs who feel valued stay longer and do better work. Remember birthdays, give bonuses, say thank you.
