A property management system (PMS) is the central software platform that runs your PM operation. It handles rent collection, accounting, maintenance tracking, tenant communication, owner reporting, and leasing — all in one place.

Choosing the wrong system costs you months of migration time and thousands in lost productivity. This guide compares every major platform so you can choose right the first time.

Detailed Platform Reviews

AppFolio Property Manager

★★★★★ 4.6/5 (industry average)

Overview: AppFolio is the most popular PM system for mid-size companies. It's a true all-in-one: accounting, maintenance, leasing, communication, reporting, and now AI-powered features. The AI leasing assistant (Lisa) handles prospect inquiries 24/7.

Strengths: Excellent mobile app. Strong accounting with trust account support. Built-in tenant screening. Online rent payments with multiple options. Smart maintenance workflows. Owner portal is clean and professional.

Weaknesses: 50-unit minimum to start. Per-unit pricing gets expensive at scale. Customer support can be slow during peak times. Some features require higher tiers.

Best for: PM companies with 50-5,000 units who want a proven, feature-rich platform and don't mind paying premium pricing.

Buildium

★★★★☆ 4.4/5

Overview: Buildium offers the best value for PMs under 200 units. The Essential plan at $55/month includes core PM features, and the Growth plan adds more automation. Owned by RealPage, so it benefits from a large ecosystem.

Strengths: Low barrier to entry. Strong tenant screening (TransUnion partnership). Good community association management features. eSignatures included. Reasonable pricing that doesn't punish growth.

Weaknesses: Accounting is good but not as robust as AppFolio or Rent Manager. Reporting customization is limited. Mobile app is functional but not great. API access is limited on lower tiers.

Best for: New PM companies or those under 200 units who want solid features without breaking the bank.

Rent Manager

★★★★★ 4.7/5

Overview: Rent Manager is the power user's choice. Extremely customizable — you can configure virtually every workflow, report, and field. Steeper learning curve but unmatched flexibility for complex operations.

Strengths: Most customizable PM system on the market. Excellent accounting (best trust accounting in the industry). Handles mixed-use portfolios (residential + commercial + affordable housing). Strong API. Dedicated support.

Weaknesses: UI feels dated compared to AppFolio/Buildium. Learning curve is significant — budget 2-4 weeks for full implementation. Custom pricing means you need to negotiate. Mobile experience is adequate but not modern.

Best for: Established PM companies with 200+ units, especially those with complex accounting needs, mixed portfolios, or affordable housing compliance requirements.

Yardi Breeze / Yardi Voyager

★★★★☆ 4.3/5 (Breeze) | ★★★★☆ 4.2/5 (Voyager)

Overview: Yardi is the 800-lb gorilla of PM software. Voyager is the enterprise system used by the largest PM companies in the world. Breeze is their simplified version for smaller operators. If you're planning to scale to 1,000+ units, starting on Yardi means never having to migrate.

Strengths: Breeze is genuinely user-friendly. Voyager is infinitely scalable. Both benefit from Yardi's ecosystem (screening, payments, energy management, marketing). Industry standard for institutional investors.

Weaknesses: Voyager implementation is expensive and time-consuming (months). Breeze lacks some features serious PMs need. Customer support quality varies. Contract terms can be inflexible.

Best for: Breeze: 50-400 units wanting Yardi credibility. Voyager: 500+ units or companies managing for institutional owners who require Yardi.

How to Choose the Right System

Use this decision framework:

Step 1: Portfolio Size

  • Under 20 units: TenantCloud (free) or RentRedi ($12/mo)
  • 20-75 units: Buildium Essential ($55/mo) or TenantCloud paid
  • 75-200 units: AppFolio or Buildium Growth
  • 200-500 units: AppFolio, Rent Manager, or Yardi Breeze
  • 500+ units: Rent Manager, Yardi Voyager, or RealPage

Step 2: Property Types

  • Single-family only: Propertyware, Buildium, or AppFolio
  • Multifamily: AppFolio, Yardi, or Rent Manager
  • Mixed use: Rent Manager (best for complex portfolios)
  • HOA/associations: Buildium or AppFolio (both have HOA modules)
  • Commercial: Yardi Voyager or MRI Software

Step 3: Must-Have Features

  • Trust accounting: Required in most states. All major platforms support it, but Rent Manager and AppFolio do it best.
  • Online payments: Every platform offers this. Check processing fees — they range from $0 to $2.50/transaction.
  • Maintenance tracking: Built into all platforms. For advanced maintenance, add Property Meld as an integration.
  • Owner portal: Essential for client retention. AppFolio and Buildium have the best owner portals.
  • Tenant screening: Most platforms partner with TransUnion or Experian. Pass the cost to applicants.
Migration tip: The #1 reason PMs stick with bad software is migration fear. But the cost of a bad system (inefficiency, errors, poor client experience) compounds every month. If your current system is holding you back, budget 4-6 weeks for migration and rip the bandaid off.

The Hidden Costs of PM Systems

Advertised pricing is never the full story. Watch for:

  • Payment processing fees: $1-2.50 per ACH transaction, 2.9% + $0.30 for credit cards
  • Screening fees: Usually passed to applicants, but some platforms take a cut
  • Implementation/setup fees: $500-5,000+ for enterprise platforms
  • Training costs: Factor in 20-40 hours of staff time for learning a new system
  • Data migration: Some platforms charge for assisted migration
  • Add-on modules: Advanced features (AI, premium reporting, marketing tools) often cost extra
