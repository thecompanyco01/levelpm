Online Property Management Training: Best Courses & Programs in 2026

Updated March 9, 2026 · 20 min read

Online property management training has exploded in the last two years. What used to require flying to a NARPM conference or sitting through a semester-long college course can now be done from your laptop in a weekend. But the quality gap between programs is massive — some are genuinely career-changing, while others are glorified PowerPoint decks with a certificate at the end.

This guide breaks down every major online property management training option in 2026, from free resources to professional certifications. Whether you're a new PM looking to break into the industry, a company owner who needs to train a growing team, or a veteran who wants to formalize what you already know — you'll find the right program here.

Why Online PM Training Beats In-Person (For Most People)

The traditional path to property management education — community college courses, IREM classrooms, NARPM regional events — still works. But online training has several concrete advantages:

  • Cost savings of 50-80% — No travel, no hotel, no missed work days. A $265 NARPM course becomes a $47-147 online equivalent.
  • Self-paced learning — Your new hire can complete onboarding training in 3 days instead of waiting for the next scheduled class in 6 weeks.
  • Immediate application — Watch a module on tenant screening, then screen your next applicant using what you just learned. In-person classes create a gap between learning and doing.
  • Scalable for teams — Train 1 person or 50 at the same cost. In-person per-seat pricing kills your training budget as you grow.

The one caveat: online training works best when it includes practical exercises, not just video lectures. Look for programs that include templates, SOPs, and real-world scenarios — not just theory.

1. IREM CPM Online Program

The Certified Property Manager (CPM) designation from the Institute of Real Estate Management remains the gold standard. Their online program covers asset management, financial analysis, maintenance, and leadership across 7 required courses plus electives. It's rigorous, respected by employers, and expensive.

Best for: Property managers who want to manage large portfolios (500+ units) or work for institutional owners (REITs, pension funds). The CPM opens doors that other certifications don't.

Drawback: At $8,000-12,000 total cost and 12-18 months commitment, it's overkill for someone managing 50-200 doors as an owner-operator.

2. NARPM Online Courses

The National Association of Residential Property Managers offers individual courses ($30-265) covering everything from marketing to maintenance. Their RMP (Residential Management Professional) and MPM (Master Property Manager) designations carry weight in residential PM.

Best for: Residential-focused PMs who want industry networking alongside education. NARPM's real value is the community, not just the coursework.

Drawback: Courses are information-heavy but light on actionable templates and SOPs. You'll learn what to do but not get plug-and-play systems.

3. Udemy Property Management Courses

Udemy hosts dozens of PM courses ranging from "Property Management 101" to specialized topics like tenant screening and maintenance coordination. Most are $15-50 (or free during sales).

Best for: Complete beginners who want to test whether PM is the right career before investing more. The "Property Management: Managing Rental Properties" course by Symon He has 20,000+ students and solid reviews.

Drawback: Quality varies wildly. Many instructors are coaches, not practicing PMs. And Udemy certificates carry zero weight with employers.

4. Buildium Academy

Free training modules for Buildium property management software users. Covers software-specific workflows plus general PM best practices. Well-produced video content with step-by-step tutorials.

Best for: Teams already using Buildium who want to maximize their software investment.

Drawback: Obviously biased toward Buildium's workflow. Not useful if you use AppFolio, Rent Manager, or another platform.

Most states require a real estate license to manage properties for others. Online pre-licensing courses from providers like CE Shop, Kaplan, and Colibri cover state-specific requirements. Prices range from $100-500 depending on the state.

Best for: Anyone who needs a license to legally operate. Non-negotiable in most states.

How to Choose the Right Online PM Training

For New Property Managers

  1. Start with your state license — If your state requires one, this is step zero. You can't legally practice without it.
  2. Take a Udemy course — Spend $15-50 to get the fundamentals. Don't overthink this step.
  3. Get hands-on experience — No course replaces managing actual properties. Work for a PM company for 6-12 months before going independent.
  4. Consider NARPM RMP — After 1-2 years of experience, the RMP designation adds credibility and connections.

For PM Company Owners

  1. Build internal SOPs first — Your team needs systems more than certificates. Download our free SOP templates to start.
  2. Invest in a scaling framework — The PM Scaling Kit or DoorGrow coaching can shortcut years of trial and error.
  3. Train your team, not just yourself — The biggest leverage is getting your team to operate independently. Look for programs with team-friendly pricing.

For Career Switchers

  1. Research your state requirements — Some states have no licensing requirements for PMs. Others require a full real estate license.
  2. Start as an assistant PM — Many companies hire assistant PMs with no experience. Use this to learn on the job while earning.
  3. Focus on certifications that employers value — CPM and RMP are the two that consistently appear in job listings.

What Good PM Training Actually Teaches

The problem with most property management training isn't the information — it's the format. Lectures about fair housing law and maintenance scheduling are necessary but insufficient. The best training programs also cover:

Systems Thinking

How to build repeatable processes that work without you. This is the difference between a PM who manages 50 doors personally and one who manages 500 through a team. It's also the hardest thing to teach, because most PM trainers are hands-on operators, not systems builders.

Financial Analysis

Understanding cap rates, NOI, and cash-on-cash return isn't just for investors — it's how you demonstrate value to owners and justify your fees. PMs who speak the language of returns keep owners longer.

Owner Acquisition

Most training programs focus on managing properties, not getting them. But for company owners, owner acquisition is the most important skill. If you can't bring in new doors, nothing else matters.

Technology Stack

AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager, Propertyware — every PM needs to master at least one platform. But beyond the core PMS, modern PMs also need skills in digital marketing, CRM management, and data analytics.

Building Your Own PM Training Program

If you're a PM company owner, the most valuable training investment isn't a course — it's building your own internal training program. Here's a framework that works at any scale:

Week 1: Foundation

  • Company overview, culture, and expectations
  • Software training (your PMS, communication tools)
  • Fair housing law review and quiz
  • Shadow an experienced PM for 2 full days

Week 2: Core Operations

  • Leasing process from showing to signed lease
  • Maintenance coordination (using your SOP)
  • Tenant communication standards
  • Handle 3 supervised tenant calls

Week 3: Advanced

  • Move-in/move-out inspection process
  • Owner reporting and communication
  • Rent collection and delinquency procedures
  • Manage 5-10 units under supervision

Week 4: Independence

  • Take ownership of 20-30 units
  • Handle first owner report independently
  • Process first lease renewal
  • 30-day performance review with manager

This 30-day ramp is how companies like Greystar and Lincoln Property train hundreds of new PMs every year. Scale it down for your team size, but keep the structure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a certification to be a property manager?

In most states, you need a real estate license but not a PM-specific certification. Certifications like CPM or RMP are voluntary and boost credibility, but they're not legally required. Check our state licensing guide for your specific requirements.

How long does online PM training take?

A basic foundation course takes 10-20 hours. A full certification program (NARPM RMP) takes 3-6 months of part-time study. Building internal company SOPs and training materials takes 2-4 weeks of focused work.

Is online PM training worth it?

If you choose the right program, absolutely. The key is selecting training that matches your goal: Udemy for exploration, NARPM for credentials, ScaleDoors for scaling systems. Avoid programs that are all theory and no templates.
