Buildium vs AppFolio 2026: Which Property Management Software Is Right for You?

Updated March 2026 · 12 min read

Choosing between Buildium and AppFolio is one of the most consequential decisions a property management company makes. The wrong software costs you hours per week in manual work, frustrates your team, and limits your ability to scale. The right one becomes invisible infrastructure that lets you add doors without adding headcount.

We've spent hundreds of hours helping PM companies scale from 50 to 500+ doors, and software choice comes up in every single engagement. Here's the honest breakdown.

Pricing Breakdown

Buildium Pricing (2026)

  • Essential: $58/mo — up to 150 units, basic features
  • Growth: $183/mo — up to 5,000 units, full features
  • Premium: $375/mo — unlimited units, priority support, Open API

Buildium's pricing is straightforward and doesn't penalize you per-unit, making it very cost-effective as you scale within a tier.

AppFolio Pricing (2026)

  • Core: ~$1.40/unit/mo (minimum 50 units = $70/mo minimum)
  • Plus: ~$3/unit/mo — advanced features, AI tools
  • Max: Custom pricing for large portfolios

AppFolio's per-unit pricing means costs scale linearly. At 200 units on Core, you're paying ~$280/mo. At 500 units on Plus, that's $1,500/mo. It gets expensive fast.

When to Choose Buildium

  • You're just starting out — Buildium's lower entry cost and easier learning curve gets you operational faster
  • You manage 100-500 units — The flat pricing tiers become extremely cost-effective at this range
  • You need integrations — Buildium's open API and Zapier integration connects to your existing tools
  • You manage HOAs/community associations — Buildium has dedicated HOA management features
  • Budget is a primary concern — Buildium is almost always cheaper above 150 units

When to Choose AppFolio

  • You're scaling aggressively past 500 units — AppFolio's infrastructure handles large portfolios better
  • You want AI automation — AppFolio's AI leasing assistant and smart maintenance save significant staff time
  • Your team is mobile-first — AppFolio's mobile app is substantially better
  • You do a lot of leasing — AI-powered showing scheduling and lead follow-up is a genuine competitive advantage
  • You want best-in-class owner reporting — AppFolio's owner portal is industry-leading

The Hidden Factor: Your Team's Processes

Here's what nobody talks about in software comparisons: the software doesn't matter if your processes are broken.

We've seen PM companies on AppFolio running less efficiently than companies on spreadsheets — because they never documented their workflows, never trained their team properly, and never standardized their operations.

The companies that scale successfully (regardless of software) share one thing: documented SOPs for every repeatable task. Maintenance intake, move-in/move-out, owner onboarding, lease renewals, vendor management — all documented, all trained, all measured.

Our Recommendation

Under 200 units: Start with Buildium Essential or Growth. It's simpler, cheaper, and gets you running fast. You can always migrate later.

200-500 units and scaling fast: This is the decision zone. If you're adding 20+ doors/month, AppFolio's automation pays for itself. If growth is steady, Buildium saves you thousands per year.

500+ units: AppFolio Plus or Max. The AI features and infrastructure justify the per-unit cost at this scale.

Regardless of choice: Invest in your processes before your software. The best software with bad processes still produces bad results.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch from Buildium to AppFolio (or vice versa)?

Yes, but it's painful. Plan 2-3 months for migration. Both offer data export, but you'll lose some historical data in the transition. This is why getting the choice right the first time matters.

Is there a free trial?

Buildium offers a 14-day free trial. AppFolio requires a demo with their sales team. Neither offers a freemium tier.

What about Rent Manager, Propertyware, or RentRedi?

Rent Manager is excellent for large portfolios (1,000+) but has a steep learning curve. Propertyware targets single-family portfolios. RentRedi is best for self-managing landlords with under 50 units. For the 50-500 door PM company, Buildium and AppFolio remain the top two choices.
