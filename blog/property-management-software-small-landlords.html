Detailed Reviews

1. Stessa BEST FREE

Best for: Landlords who want financial tracking, tax prep, and portfolio analytics — for free.

Stessa (acquired by Roofstock) focuses on the financial side of rental property ownership. It automatically imports transactions from linked bank accounts, categorizes expenses, tracks income, and generates tax-ready reports. If your biggest headache is bookkeeping and tax time, Stessa solves it at zero cost.

What it does well:

  • Automatic transaction import and categorization
  • Net cash flow dashboards per property
  • Tax-ready financial reports (Schedule E)
  • Portfolio performance tracking
  • Document storage for leases and receipts

What it doesn't do: Tenant screening, online rent collection, maintenance tracking, lease management. It's a financial tool, not a full PM platform.

Price: Free for core features. Stessa Pro ($20/month) adds additional reporting and market analytics.

Best paired with: Avail for tenant-facing features (listings, screening, rent collection).

2. Avail (by Apartments.com) BEST FOR DIY LANDLORDS

Best for: Self-managing landlords who need listings, screening, and rent collection.

Avail covers the tenant-facing side of landlording. List vacancies (syndicated to Apartments.com, Zillow, Trulia), screen applicants (credit, criminal, eviction), sign leases online, and collect rent — all from one platform. The free tier is genuinely useful; the paid tier ($7/unit/month) adds customization.

What it does well:

  • Vacancy listing syndication to major sites
  • Tenant screening (credit, background, eviction)
  • State-specific lease templates
  • Online rent collection (ACH)
  • Maintenance request tracking

What it doesn't do: Advanced accounting, trust accounts, owner reporting, vendor management. It's a landlord tool, not a PM company tool.

Price: Free (basic). Unlimited Plus at $7/unit/month adds next-day rent payments, custom applications, and no tenant screening fees.

3. TenantCloud BEST ALL-IN-ONE FREE

Best for: Landlords who want a full PM platform without paying (up to 75 units).

TenantCloud offers the most complete free tier in the market — rent collection, accounting, maintenance tracking, tenant screening, and lease management for up to 75 units at no cost. The interface is functional if not beautiful, and it covers 90% of what a small landlord needs.

What it does well:

  • Full PM workflow in one platform (free up to 75 units)
  • Online rent collection and payment tracking
  • Built-in accounting with QuickBooks integration
  • Maintenance request portal
  • Tenant and owner portals

Limitations: The free tier has ads, limited support, and basic reporting. Paid plans ($15-50/month) remove restrictions. The UI feels dated compared to newer platforms.

4. RentRedi

Best for: Landlords who manage from their phone.

RentRedi is built mobile-first — the app is genuinely good, not an afterthought. At $12/month (annual plan), it's the cheapest paid platform with unlimited units. Features include rent collection, tenant screening, lease signing, maintenance tracking, and even a landlord banking integration.

Standout feature: Mobile-first design. If you manage on the go and hate desktop dashboards, RentRedi is the pick.

Price: $12/month (annual) or $20/month (monthly). Unlimited units included.

5. Buildium BEST FOR GROWTH

Best for: Landlords planning to scale to 50-200+ units or start a PM company.

Buildium is a professional-grade PM platform that's accessible enough for small landlords but powerful enough to grow into. It includes everything: accounting with trust accounts, tenant and owner portals, maintenance management, lease tracking, and 1099 e-filing. If you think you might start a PM company someday, start with Buildium.

What it does well:

  • Full trust accounting (required for managing others' properties)
  • Owner and tenant portals
  • Maintenance workflow with vendor management
  • Comprehensive financial reporting
  • 1099 electronic filing
  • Website builder for PM companies

Price: Essential plan starts at $58/month for up to 150 units. Growth plan at $183/month adds priority support and additional features. See our full Buildium vs. AppFolio comparison.

6. AppFolio

Best for: PM companies or landlords with 50+ units who need enterprise features.

AppFolio is one of the most powerful PM platforms on the market, but it has a 50-unit minimum and per-unit pricing that makes it expensive for small landlords. If you're already at 50+ units, AppFolio's AI-powered features, robust accounting, and polished interface are hard to beat.

Price: ~$1.40/unit/month with a minimum of $280/month (200-unit minimum on some plans). Not practical for landlords under 50 units. See our AppFolio vs. Buildium comparison.

7. Rent Manager

Best for: Power users who want deep customization and control.

Rent Manager is highly customizable — you can configure virtually every workflow, report, and screen. This flexibility is powerful for experienced PM operators but overwhelming for small landlords. The interface has a steeper learning curve than Buildium or AppFolio.

Price: Custom pricing based on portfolio. Contact for a quote. Generally competitive with Buildium and AppFolio on a per-unit basis.

8. Hemlane

Best for: Landlords who want to self-manage with professional backup.

Hemlane's unique angle is a hybrid model — you manage day-to-day, but Hemlane's team handles after-hours maintenance calls and can coordinate repairs. It's like having a PM company on standby without paying full management fees.

Price: Basic at $30/month for up to 1 unit (plus $2/unit). Complete plan at $68/month includes repair coordination and local agent matching.

9. Rentec Direct

Best for: Budget-conscious landlords who need professional-grade features.

Rentec Direct offers solid PM functionality at lower prices than Buildium or AppFolio. Trust accounting, tenant screening, online payments, and 1099 filing are all included. The interface is functional but not as polished as newer competitors.

Price: Rentec PM starts at $45/month for up to 100 units. One of the best price-to-feature ratios in the market.

10. DoorLoop

Best for: Landlords who prioritize modern design and ease of use.

DoorLoop is a newer entrant with a clean, modern interface. It covers all the basics — rent collection, accounting, maintenance, leasing — with a UI that's noticeably easier to navigate than legacy platforms. Good for small landlords who want professional software without the learning curve.

Price: Starter at $59/month for up to 20 units. Pro at $89/month, Premium at $139/month with additional features.

Which Software Should You Choose?

1-5 Units: Go Free

Use Stessa (financial tracking) + Avail (tenant management) together. Both are free and cover different needs. Total cost: $0. This combo handles 90% of what a small landlord needs.

5-20 Units: Add Paid Features

Either upgrade to TenantCloud paid ($15/month) for an all-in-one solution, or use RentRedi ($12/month) if you prefer mobile management. Both are affordable enough that the time savings justify the cost at this scale.

20-50 Units: Go Professional

At this scale, you need proper accounting, maintenance workflows, and potentially trust accounts (if managing for others). Buildium ($58/month) or Rentec Direct ($45/month) are the best options. The investment is roughly $1-3 per unit per month — well worth it for the operational efficiency.

50+ Units: Enterprise Time

Once you're past 50 units, you're running a business, not a side hustle. AppFolio, Buildium Growth, or Rent Manager are your platforms. At this stage, software is a tiny percentage of revenue and the right platform makes a massive difference in scalability.

What to Consider Before Choosing

Your Growth Plans Matter Most

The biggest regret landlords have with PM software is choosing a platform they outgrow in 12 months. If you're at 10 units and plan to stay there, free tools are fine. If you're at 10 units and want to hit 100, start with Buildium now — migrating later is painful.

Online Rent Collection Is Non-Negotiable

In 2026, there's no excuse for collecting rent via check or Venmo. Every platform on this list offers online rent collection with ACH direct deposit. This single feature reduces late payments by 40-60% according to multiple PM software companies' data.

Accounting Matters More Than You Think

You don't need fancy accounting... until tax time. Or until you have a dispute with a tenant over security deposits. Or until you want to show a lender your rental income history. Proper property management bookkeeping saves thousands in the long run. If your free tool doesn't handle it, pair it with Stessa.

Don't Pay for Features You Won't Use

Enterprise features like CAM reconciliation, multi-entity accounting, investor portals, and commercial lease management are irrelevant for a landlord with 10 single-family rentals. Don't pay for Buildium Premium when Buildium Essential does everything you need.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free property management software for small landlords?

Stessa is the best free option for financial tracking and tax preparation. TenantCloud offers a free tier for up to 75 units with basic management features. Avail provides free landlord tools for listings, applications, and lease signing. For most small landlords, Stessa plus Avail covers the basics at no cost.

How much does property management software cost for small landlords?

Costs range from free (Stessa, Avail, TenantCloud basic) to $1-3 per unit per month for professional tools like Buildium, AppFolio, or RentRedi. For a small landlord with 10 units, expect to pay $0-30 per month depending on the platform and features you need.

Do I need property management software for just one rental?

Not strictly necessary, but free tools like Stessa (financial tracking) and Avail (tenant screening, lease signing) save time and reduce errors. As soon as you have 2+ units, software becomes almost essential for staying organized and compliant.

What features should small landlords look for in PM software?

Essential features: online rent collection, tenant screening, lease management, maintenance request tracking, and financial reporting. Nice-to-have: vacancy listing syndication, document storage, and tax preparation. Skip enterprise features unless you plan to scale significantly.

Should I use property management software or hire a property manager?

For 1-10 units, software is usually sufficient if you have the time. At 10-20+ units, consider a property manager — the time savings justify the 8-12% management fee. Some landlords use both: PM software for oversight plus a PM company for execution.

Can I switch property management software later?

Yes, but it involves migrating tenant data, financial history, leases, and documents. Most platforms offer import tools, but expect 1-2 weeks of setup work. Choose carefully upfront — the switching cost is time and risk, not money.
