Property Management Inspection Checklists: Move-In, Move-Out & Annual

100+ line items covering every room and system. Protect owners from liability and tenants from unfair charges.

Home → Blog → Inspection Checklists

Inspections are your primary legal protection in property management. A thorough, documented inspection protects against: security deposit disputes (the #1 source of PM lawsuits), undiscovered property damage, safety liability, and insurance claim denials.

There are three types of inspections every PM company must master.

1. Move-In Inspection Checklist

Conducted with the tenant present, before they take possession. Both parties sign. This is your baseline document — every future claim references this.

2. Move-Out Inspection

Compare against the move-in report. Focus on changes that exceed normal wear and tear.

Normal Wear vs. Tenant Damage

Normal Wear (Not Chargeable)Tenant Damage (Chargeable)
Small nail holes in wallsLarge holes, unauthorized modifications
Faded paint from sunlightCrayon, marker, paint splatters
Worn carpet in traffic areasStains, burns, pet damage, tears
Loose door handles from useBroken doors, missing hardware
Minor scuffs on hardwoodDeep scratches, water damage, gouges
Aging appliance appearanceBroken appliance parts, missing racks

3. Annual Inspection Focus Areas

Annual inspections focus on property condition and safety — not tenant housekeeping (though note any lease violations):

  • Safety devices: Test all smoke detectors, CO detectors. Replace batteries or units as needed.
  • Water damage: Check under sinks, around toilets, ceiling stains, window condensation
  • HVAC: Filter condition, system operation, unusual noises
  • Plumbing: Slow drains, dripping faucets, toilet operation, water heater condition
  • Pest evidence: Droppings, nests, damage, entry points
  • Exterior: Roof (visible), gutters, siding, foundation cracks, grading
  • Lease compliance: Unauthorized occupants, pets, modifications, storage
