Step-by-Step: How to Get Licensed in Utah

  1. Complete pre-license education — 48 hours PM-specific education. Available online through approved providers.
  2. Pass the state exam — 100 questions with a 70% passing score required.
  3. Submit your application — Apply through Utah Division of Real Estate (DRE) with your exam results, fingerprints, and background check.
  4. Find a sponsoring broker — Recommended for mentorship and MLS access.
  5. Get your license activated — Once approved, your license must be activated before you can practice.

Tips for Success as a Property Manager in Utah

  1. Know your local landlord-tenant laws — Utah has specific regulations that differ from other states. Stay current on security deposit limits, eviction procedures, and fair housing requirements.
  2. Join your state NARPM chapter — National Association of Residential Property Managers has local chapters in most states. Great for networking and continuing education.
  3. Invest in property management software — Tools like AppFolio, Buildium, or RentManager will help you scale efficiently.
  4. Build SOPs early — Standardized processes for maintenance, tenant screening, move-ins, and owner reporting are essential for scaling beyond 50 doors.
  5. Focus on owner acquisition — The #1 growth lever for PM companies is getting more property owners to trust you with their properties.
