Best Property Management CRM Software in 2026: Complete Comparison

Updated March 2026 · 16 min read

Most property management companies don't have a CRM. They track owner leads in spreadsheets, sticky notes, or their head. That works at 20 doors. At 100+, it's a recipe for lost deals and forgotten follow-ups.

A good CRM for property management doesn't just store contacts — it automates your owner acquisition pipeline, tracks every touchpoint, and tells you exactly which deals need attention. Here's how the top options compare.

1. LeadSimple — Best Overall for PM Companies

LeadSimple was built specifically for property management companies. It's the only CRM that truly understands the PM sales cycle — from first contact with a property owner to signed management agreement and ongoing account management.

Key Features

  • PM-specific pipeline stages: Lead → Consultation → Proposal → Agreement → Onboarding
  • Automated follow-up sequences: Set it and forget it — the CRM follows up for you
  • Process management: Build repeatable workflows for owner onboarding, renewals, move-ins
  • Integration with PM software: Connects to AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager
  • Reporting: Pipeline value, conversion rates, revenue forecasting
  • Built-in calling and texting

✅ Purpose-built for PM companies — no customization needed
✅ Excellent automation and follow-up sequences
✅ Process management goes beyond traditional CRM
✅ Strong integration with PM software

❌ Higher price point ($65/user/mo)
❌ Overkill for very small operations (under 50 doors)
❌ Learning curve for process builder

Our verdict: If you're serious about growing your PM company through owner acquisition, LeadSimple is the gold standard. The ROI is clear: one extra signed owner per month more than covers the cost. Best for PM companies managing 100+ doors with active growth plans.

2. HubSpot CRM — Best Free Option

HubSpot's free CRM is genuinely useful and incredibly generous. For PM companies just starting to formalize their sales process, it's hard to beat "free."

Key Features

  • Contact management: Unlimited contacts and companies
  • Deal pipeline: Visual drag-and-drop pipeline (customizable stages)
  • Email tracking: Know when prospects open your emails
  • Meeting scheduler: Let owners book consultations directly
  • Forms and landing pages: Capture leads from your website
  • Email marketing: Up to 2,000 emails/month on free plan

✅ Free tier is genuinely excellent
✅ Beautiful, intuitive interface
✅ Massive ecosystem of integrations
✅ Built-in marketing tools (email, forms, landing pages)

❌ Not PM-specific — requires customization
❌ Paid plans get expensive fast ($20 → $890+/mo)
❌ No integration with PM software out of the box

Our verdict: Perfect starting point for PM companies with 0-100 doors who don't want to pay for a CRM yet. You'll outgrow the free tier eventually, but it buys you time to build a sales process before investing in something PM-specific.

3. Buildium — Best All-in-One

If you already use Buildium for property management, adding their CRM features makes sense. It's not a standalone CRM — it's CRM functionality baked into your PM software.

Key Features

  • Lead tracking: Track owner leads alongside your existing portfolio
  • Website builder: Professional PM website with lead capture
  • Tenant screening integration: TransUnion screening built in
  • Owner portal: Existing owners can access reports and statements
  • Maintenance tracking: Full work order management

✅ Everything in one platform — no integration headaches
✅ Good for companies that want simplicity
✅ Reasonable pricing for smaller portfolios
✅ Strong owner portal and reporting

❌ CRM features are basic compared to dedicated CRMs
❌ Limited automation for sales follow-up
❌ Pipeline management is rudimentary
❌ Pricing jumps significantly at scale

Our verdict: Best for PM companies under 150 doors who want one tool for everything. If owner acquisition is a serious growth channel for you, you'll eventually need a dedicated CRM alongside Buildium.

4. AppFolio — Best for Larger Operations

AppFolio's CRM-like features are embedded in their comprehensive PM platform. Their lead management and marketing tools are designed for companies managing 200+ units.

Key Features

  • Lead management: Track and nurture owner and tenant leads
  • AI-powered features: Smart maintenance categorization, automated responses
  • Marketing website: Professional sites with lead capture forms
  • Owner statements and reports: Institutional-quality financial reporting
  • Vacancy posting: Syndicate listings to Zillow, Apartments.com, etc.

✅ Enterprise-grade platform with modern UI
✅ AI features are genuinely useful
✅ Excellent for multifamily and mixed portfolios
✅ Strong vacancy marketing tools

❌ $280/month minimum — expensive for small operators
❌ CRM is secondary to PM operations
❌ Less flexible than dedicated CRMs
❌ Requires 200+ unit minimum

Our verdict: If you're already on AppFolio, use their built-in lead tools. But for serious owner acquisition at scale, pair AppFolio with LeadSimple (they integrate natively).

5. Pipedrive — Best for Sales-Driven PMs

Pipedrive isn't made for property management, but its pure sales focus makes it excellent for PM companies that treat owner acquisition as a structured sales process.

Key Features

  • Visual pipeline: The best drag-and-drop pipeline in the business
  • Activity-based selling: Focus on actions, not just deals
  • Automation: Trigger emails, create activities, move deals automatically
  • Mobile app: Full CRM access on your phone (great for field work)
  • Reporting: Conversion rates, deal velocity, revenue forecasting

✅ Best-in-class pipeline management
✅ Very affordable ($14/user/mo)
✅ Intuitive — minimal training needed
✅ Strong mobile app for on-the-go PMs

❌ Not PM-specific — requires custom setup
❌ No PM software integrations
❌ Marketing features are add-ons
❌ Limited process management

Our verdict: Best budget option for PM companies that want a real CRM without the PM-specific price tag. If you're comfortable customizing pipeline stages and fields, Pipedrive delivers enormous value at $14/month.

5 Questions Before You Buy

  1. How many owner leads per month? Under 5? A spreadsheet might be fine for now. Over 10? You need a CRM.
  2. Do you have a sales process? A CRM enforces a process. If you don't have one, define it first (we cover this in our Scaling Kit).
  3. What PM software do you use? Integration matters. LeadSimple + AppFolio = seamless. Random CRM + random PM software = manual data entry.
  4. Who will use it? If it's just you, simplicity wins. If it's a team, you need permissions, reporting, and activity tracking.
  5. What's your growth target? Planning to add 50 doors this year? Invest in LeadSimple. Maintaining current portfolio? HubSpot Free is sufficient.

CRM Implementation Tips for PMs

  1. Define your pipeline stages first: Lead → Qualified → Consultation → Proposal → Negotiation → Signed → Onboarding
  2. Set up automation day one: Auto-send a follow-up email 3 days after proposal delivery. This alone will increase close rates.
  3. Track the right metrics: Lead-to-consultation rate, proposal-to-close rate, average deal cycle time, revenue per new owner
  4. Log every interaction: Calls, emails, in-person meetings. The CRM only works if the data is in it.
  5. Review weekly: Every Monday, look at your pipeline. Which deals are stale? Who needs a follow-up? Where are leads getting stuck?
