Property Management Software Comparison 2026: AppFolio vs Buildium vs Rent Manager

Updated March 2026 · 22 min read

Choosing property management software is one of the highest-stakes decisions a PM company makes. Switch costs are brutal — migrating data, retraining staff, and disrupting operations can cost 3-6 months of productivity. So you want to get it right the first time.

We've analyzed the top 8 PM software platforms based on features, pricing, ease of use, and real user feedback to help you make the right choice for your portfolio size and business model.

AppFolio — Best for Growing PM Companies

AppFolio is the gold standard for professional property management companies managing 200+ units. It's a complete platform with some of the best AI features in the industry.

What Makes It Stand Out

  • AI Leasing Assistant: Responds to prospect inquiries 24/7, schedules tours, and follows up automatically
  • Smart Maintenance: AI categorizes and routes maintenance requests to the right vendor
  • Modern UI: Beautiful, intuitive interface that doesn't feel like software from 2010
  • Investor Portal: Institutional-quality reporting for property owners
  • Integrated Screening: Credit, criminal, eviction all in one click
  • Online Payments: ACH and credit card rent collection

Pricing

  • Core: $1.40/unit/month (200 unit minimum = $280/mo)
  • Plus: $3/unit/month (more AI features, workflow automation)
  • Max: $5/unit/month (priority support, advanced reporting)

Pros: Best AI features, modern UI, excellent tenant/owner portals, strong marketing tools, regular feature updates

Cons: 200-unit minimum prices out small operators, per-unit pricing adds up at scale, some features require higher tiers, limited customization

🏆 Best for: Professional PM companies managing 200-5,000+ units who want a modern, feature-rich platform and are willing to pay for quality. The AI features alone can save 10-20 hours/month.

Buildium — Best for Small to Mid-Size PMs

Buildium hits the sweet spot for PM companies managing 50-500 units. It's feature-rich enough for professional operations but accessible enough for growing companies.

What Makes It Stand Out

  • No unit minimum: Start from day one, even with a small portfolio
  • Built-in website: Professional PM website with lead capture
  • Performance insights: Benchmark your performance against industry averages
  • 1099 e-filing: Automated tax form generation for owners and vendors
  • Community association management: Great for HOA/condo associations

Pricing

  • Essential: $55/mo (up to 150 units)
  • Growth: $174/mo (up to 150 units + marketing, inspections, priority support)
  • Premium: $375/mo (up to 500 units + all features)

Pros: No unit minimum, all-in-one platform, good owner/tenant portals, solid accounting, HOA management, free trial available

Cons: UI feels dated compared to AppFolio, pricing jumps at tier boundaries, customer support can be slow, limited AI features

🏆 Best for: PM companies with 50-500 units who need a complete platform without AppFolio's unit minimum. Also excellent for companies managing HOAs alongside rentals.

Rent Manager — Best for Complex Portfolios

Rent Manager is the power user's choice. It's the most customizable PM software on the market, with depth that AppFolio and Buildium can't match — but that flexibility comes with a steeper learning curve.

What Makes It Stand Out

  • Extreme customization: Custom fields, reports, workflows for virtually anything
  • Advanced accounting: Full double-entry accounting with GL, AP, AR, bank rec
  • Mixed portfolio support: Residential, commercial, student housing, storage all in one
  • rmAppSuite: Mobile inspection, maintenance, and leasing apps
  • API access: Open API for integrations and custom development

Pros: Most customizable platform, best accounting engine, handles complex mixed-use portfolios, open API, no unit minimum, desktop + cloud options

Cons: Steepest learning curve, UI is functional but not beautiful, pricing is custom (opaque), implementation takes longer, less intuitive for new staff

🏆 Best for: PM companies with complex, mixed-use portfolios (residential + commercial + HOA) who need advanced accounting and are willing to invest in training. Also great for companies that want maximum customization.

Migration Tips

Switching PM software is painful. Here's how to minimize the disruption:

  1. Plan 3 months ahead. Data migration, training, and parallel testing all take time.
  2. Export everything first. Tenant data, owner data, financial records, documents, maintenance history.
  3. Don't switch mid-month. Start on the 1st of a new month for clean accounting.
  4. Run parallel for 1 month. Keep the old system as read-only backup while you verify the new system.
  5. Train your team before go-live. Not after. Schedule dedicated training sessions.
  6. Communicate to owners and tenants. New portal URLs, new payment setup, etc.
