Detailed Reviews

AppFolio

The industry standard for growing PM companies

⭐⭐⭐⭐½ (4.5/5)

Pricing: $1.49/unit/month (Core), $3.20/unit/month (Plus), $5.00/unit/month (Max). Minimum 50 units. Onboarding fee varies.

AppFolio is the most popular PM software in the industry, and for good reason. It's a complete platform that handles everything from tenant screening to financial reporting. The mobile app is excellent, the UI is modern, and they've added AI features for automated maintenance responses and smart listing descriptions.

✅ Pros

  • Best-in-class mobile app
  • Intuitive, modern interface
  • Strong AI/automation features
  • Excellent listing syndication
  • Built-in tenant screening
  • Great customer support

❌ Cons

  • 50-unit minimum (excludes small operators)
  • Expensive for smaller portfolios
  • Onboarding fees can be steep
  • Limited customization in reports
  • Can't export data easily (lock-in)
Best for: PM companies with 50-5,000 units who want an all-in-one platform and don't mind paying premium pricing. If you're scaling aggressively, AppFolio grows with you.

Buildium

The accessible choice for small to mid-size PMs

⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4.0/5)

Pricing: Essential ($58/mo for up to 150 units), Growth ($183/mo for up to 5,000 units), Premium ($375/mo). Per-unit pricing decreases with volume.

Buildium is the go-to for PM companies that aren't big enough for AppFolio's 50-unit minimum or don't want to pay AppFolio's per-unit pricing. It covers all the basics well and has a particularly strong HOA/community association management module.

✅ Pros

  • No minimum unit requirement
  • Affordable for small portfolios
  • Strong HOA management features
  • Good tenant and owner portals
  • Built-in marketing website
  • Free trial available

❌ Cons

  • Interface feels dated vs. AppFolio
  • Mobile app is basic
  • Customer support can be slow
  • Limited integrations
  • Reporting is adequate but not deep
Best for: PM companies with 1-500 units, especially those managing HOAs or community associations. Great entry point for companies transitioning from spreadsheets.

Rent Manager

The power tool for accounting-heavy operations

⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4.0/5)

Pricing: Custom pricing based on portfolio size. Generally competitive for larger portfolios (500+ units). On-premise and cloud options.

Rent Manager is the accountant's choice. If you manage a large portfolio and need serious financial reporting, trust accounting, and customizable workflows, Rent Manager is unmatched. The learning curve is steep, but the depth is incredible.

✅ Pros

  • Best-in-class accounting features
  • Highly customizable workflows
  • Powerful reporting engine
  • Handles complex portfolio structures
  • Strong trust accounting
  • On-premise option (if needed)

❌ Cons

  • Steep learning curve
  • UI feels enterprise/dated
  • Limited listing syndication
  • Requires training investment
  • Less intuitive than competitors
Best for: Established PM companies with 500+ units who prioritize financial accuracy, trust accounting, and customizable reporting over UI polish.

RentVine

The modern challenger focused on PM companies

⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4.0/5)

Pricing: $1.00/unit/month. No minimum units. Transparent pricing.

RentVine is the newer entrant that's winning converts from AppFolio and Buildium. Built specifically for third-party property managers (not landlords), it has a clean interface and aggressive pricing. It's not as feature-complete as AppFolio yet, but it's improving rapidly.

✅ Pros

  • Transparent, affordable pricing ($1/unit)
  • No minimum units
  • Clean, modern interface
  • Built for PM companies specifically
  • Good owner portal
  • Responsive to feature requests

❌ Cons

  • Newer platform, less proven
  • Fewer integrations than AppFolio
  • Accounting less mature
  • Smaller support team
  • Community/HOA features limited
Best for: PM companies with 50-1,000 units looking for AppFolio-quality UX at lower cost. Worth a serious look if you're starting fresh or considering a switch.

How to Choose: Decision Framework

  1. Portfolio size: Under 50 units → Buildium or TenantCloud. 50-500 → AppFolio or RentVine. 500+ → Rent Manager or AppFolio Plus.
  2. Budget: Tight budget → RentVine ($1/unit) or TenantCloud (free tier). Mid-range → Buildium. Premium → AppFolio.
  3. HOA management: If you manage HOAs, Buildium wins with purpose-built features.
  4. Accounting needs: Complex trust accounting or GAAP compliance → Rent Manager.
  5. Growth trajectory: Planning to scale aggressively → AppFolio (best mobile app, AI features, industry standard).
  6. Tech comfort: Want simplicity → AppFolio or RentVine. Want power → Rent Manager.
💡 Migration tip: Switching PM software is painful (3-6 months to fully transition). Choose carefully the first time. Request a full demo, do a trial period, and talk to at least 3 PM companies who use the platform before committing. The cheapest option today might cost you more in switching costs later.

Software Isn't Enough: You Need SOPs Too

Even the best PM software is only as good as the processes around it. Software handles the "what" — SOPs handle the "how." Your team needs documented procedures for:

  • How to process a new maintenance request in [your software]
  • How to run and review a tenant screening report
  • How to generate and send monthly owner statements
  • How to handle a lease violation notification
  • How to onboard a new property into the system

Without SOPs, every team member uses the software differently, and you get inconsistent results.
