Best Property Management Software in 2026: Complete Comparison

AppFolio vs Buildium vs RentManager vs Propertyware — features, pricing, and which one fits your portfolio size.

Home → Blog → PM Software Comparison

Choosing property management software is one of the most important decisions you'll make. It's your operating system — accounting, tenant portals, maintenance tracking, owner reporting, and leasing all run through it. Switch costs are brutal (migrating 100+ properties is a nightmare), so get it right the first time.

We've analyzed the top 7 PM software platforms to help you choose.

AppFolio — Best for Growth-Focused PM Companies

AppFolio is the market leader for a reason. It's the most complete platform with the best user experience. If you're serious about scaling past 50 units, AppFolio is the default choice.

Strengths

  • All-in-one platform: Accounting, leasing, maintenance, owner/tenant portals, marketing, screening — everything in one place
  • AI-powered features: Smart maintenance (AI categorization), automated rent estimates, AI listing descriptions
  • Online payments: ACH and credit card rent collection with auto-pay enrollment
  • Marketing: Built-in listing syndication to Zillow, Apartments.com, Rent.com and 50+ sites
  • Mobile app: Excellent mobile experience for field staff, maintenance, and inspections

Weaknesses

  • 50-unit minimum: Not accessible for startups with <50 doors
  • Price: $1.40/unit/month is the highest in the market. At 100 units = $140/month
  • Per-transaction fees: Charges for screening, payments, and some other features on top of base price
  • Long contracts: Annual contract with early termination fees
💡 Our verdict: If you have 50+ units and plan to grow, AppFolio is the best choice. The 50-unit minimum means you need to use something else until you get there, then migrate. Worth the switch cost.

Buildium — Best for Small to Mid-Size PMs

Buildium hits the sweet spot for PM companies between 10-500 units. No minimums, reasonable pricing, and enough features to run a professional operation.

Strengths

  • No unit minimums: Start from day one, scale as you grow
  • Great accounting: Solid trust accounting, owner statements, 1099 filing
  • Tenant screening: Built-in TransUnion screening ($15-25/application)
  • Owner portal: Professional owner-facing interface for statements and reports
  • Association management: Also handles HOA/condo associations (bonus revenue stream)

Weaknesses

  • UI feels dated compared to AppFolio
  • Marketing tools are more basic — less listing syndication
  • Mobile app is functional but not as polished
  • Pricing jumps at tier boundaries can feel steep

RentManager — Best for Established Operations

RentManager is the power user's choice. Deeply customizable, comprehensive reporting, and handles complex portfolio structures that other tools can't.

Best For

  • PM companies with 100+ units who want maximum control
  • Mixed portfolios (residential + commercial + associations)
  • Companies that need custom reporting and workflows
  • Teams that will invest time in configuration for long-term payoff

Propertyware — Best for Single-Family Specialists

Owned by RealPage, Propertyware is specifically designed for single-family rental management. If your portfolio is 100% SFR, it has specialized workflows other tools lack.

Caveat: 250-unit minimum makes it inaccessible for smaller companies. Only consider once you're at 250+ doors.

Budget Options for Startups

TenantCloud (Free-$15/mo)

Best free option. Handles basics: rent collection, maintenance requests, basic accounting. Use this from 0-50 doors, then graduate to Buildium or AppFolio.

RentRedi ($12/mo)

Mobile-first platform for solo landlords. Not designed for PM companies managing properties for others. Limited accounting and no trust account management.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

  • Per-unit minimums: AppFolio ($280/mo min) and Propertyware ($250/mo min) charge even if you have fewer units
  • Transaction fees: Most charge per screening ($15-40), per payment processing (1-3%), per listing syndication
  • Onboarding fees: Some charge $500-$2,000 for setup and data migration
  • Annual contracts: Early termination fees if you want to switch
  • Add-on features: Some platforms gate features behind higher tiers (e-signatures, inspections, texting)
