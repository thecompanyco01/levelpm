How to Choose the Perfect Name: 7-Step Process

1. Define Your Brand Position

Before brainstorming names, answer: Who is your ideal client? What price point are you? What's your personality — corporate and serious or modern and approachable? Your name should match.

2. Check Domain Availability

Your .com domain matters. If [yourname].com isn't available, try [yourname]pm.com or [yourname]properties.com. Avoid hyphens, numbers, or obscure TLDs. Check at Namecheap or GoDaddy.

3. Search the USPTO Trademark Database

Go to USPTO TESS and search your name. Look for active trademarks in Class 36 (real estate services). This 5-minute check can save you a $50,000 lawsuit.

4. Check State Business Registrations

Search your state's Secretary of State website for existing business names. Most states won't let you register a name that's already taken or too similar to an existing one.

5. Test It Out Loud

Say it on the phone. Spell it out. If you have to spell it more than once, it's too complex. The best PM company names are instantly speakable and spellable.

6. Check Social Media Handles

Search for your name on Facebook, Instagram, Google Business Profile, and LinkedIn. Consistent handles across platforms builds credibility.

7. Get Feedback from 5 People

Show the name to 5 people in your target market (property owners, not friends/family). Ask: "What kind of company do you think this is?" If they say "property management" without you telling them, you've got a winner.

The Psychology of PM Company Names

Research shows property owners choose managers based on perceived trustworthiness. Names that include words like "partner," "guardian," "premier," or "trusted" score higher on trust surveys than abstract or playful names.

For residential PM, warm names outperform cold/corporate ones. For commercial PM, the opposite is true — institutional-sounding names win.
