How to Choose Your PM Company Name: 7-Step Process

  1. Define your market position. Are you premium? Budget? Tech-forward? Your name should match your positioning.
  2. Check domain availability. Your name means nothing if you can't get the .com. Check on Namecheap or GoDaddy. Also check .co and .io as alternatives.
  3. Search your state's business registry. Make sure no one else has registered the name in your state. Check your Secretary of State's business name database.
  4. Search the USPTO trademark database. Go to tmsearch.uspto.gov and search for any existing trademarks. Even if a name isn't registered in your state, a federal trademark could cause problems.
  5. Check social media handles. Search Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn for the name. Consistent branding across platforms matters.
  6. Say it out loud 10 times. Is it easy to pronounce? Can you say it on the phone clearly? Will owners remember it? If someone hears it, can they spell it?
  7. Ask 5 property owners. Show them your top 3 choices and ask which company they'd most likely hire. Real feedback beats your gut feeling.

Names to Avoid

  • Names that are too long — "Superior Residential Property Management Solutions Inc." No one will remember this.
  • Names with numbers — "1st Choice PM" looks cheap and is hard to find online.
  • Names too similar to competitors — If there's already a "Summit PM" in your market, pick something else.
  • Overly generic names — "Property Management Company" tells no one anything and won't rank in search.
  • Hard to spell names — If you have to spell it every time on the phone, it's the wrong name.
