How to Check if a Name is Available

  1. Domain availability: Check on Namecheap, GoDaddy, or Porkbun. Aim for a .com domain.
  2. State business registry: Search your state's Secretary of State database for existing business names.
  3. Trademark search: Check the USPTO trademark database (tess2.uspto.gov) to avoid conflicts.
  4. Social media: Check if the name is available on Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Google Business Profile.
  5. Google search: Simply Google the name to see if anyone else is using it in your market.

Tips for Choosing the Perfect PM Company Name

  1. Keep it short: 2-3 words maximum. Long names get abbreviated anyway.
  2. Make it easy to spell: If you have to spell it out on the phone, it's too complicated.
  3. Avoid personal names: "Smith Property Management" is hard to sell if you ever want to exit.
  4. Think about growth: Don't box yourself into one city if you plan to expand regionally.
  5. Test it out loud: Say "Hi, I'm calling from [Name]" — does it sound professional?
  6. Check the acronym: Make sure the initials don't spell something unfortunate.

What Makes a Property Management Name Stand Out?

The best PM company names share three qualities:

  • Memorability: Property owners should remember your name after hearing it once.
  • Trust signals: Words like "partners," "solutions," "group," and "management" convey professionalism.
  • Differentiation: In a sea of "[City] Property Management" companies, a unique name helps you stand out.
