Property Management Maintenance: Complete Operations Guide

Maintenance is where PM companies make or break their reputation. A systematic approach saves owners money, keeps tenants happy, and protects your management contracts.

The Maintenance Priority Matrix

Every maintenance request falls into one of four categories. Train your team to classify instantly:

🔴 Emergency (Response: Immediate, under 2 hours)

  • Gas leak or gas smell
  • Fire or fire damage
  • Flooding or burst pipes
  • No heat when temperature is below 40°F
  • Complete electrical failure
  • Sewage backup
  • Security breach (broken lock, broken window)
  • Carbon monoxide alarm sounding

Action: Dispatch immediately. No owner approval needed. Contact owner within 1 hour to inform.

🟡 Urgent (Response: 24-48 hours)

  • HVAC not working (non-emergency temperatures)
  • Water heater failure
  • Refrigerator/freezer not cooling
  • Toilet not flushing (only toilet in unit)
  • Significant water leak (contained but ongoing)
  • Washer/dryer broken (if owner-provided)
  • Garage door not opening

Action: Schedule vendor within 24 hours. Approve repairs under maintenance threshold without owner approval.

🟢 Routine (Response: 3-7 business days)

  • Dripping faucet
  • Running toilet (still flushes)
  • Minor cosmetic damage
  • Cabinet/drawer hardware loose
  • Light switch or outlet not working
  • Caulking needs replacement
  • Screen door damaged

Action: Batch with other routine requests. Schedule vendor for efficient multi-stop visits.

🔵 Scheduled/Preventive (Response: Planned)

  • HVAC filter replacement (quarterly)
  • Gutter cleaning (biannual)
  • Pest control treatment (quarterly)
  • Landscape maintenance (weekly/biweekly)
  • Smoke detector battery replacement (annual)
  • Dryer vent cleaning (annual)
  • Exterior painting (every 5-7 years)

Action: Calendar-based. No tenant request needed. Notify tenant of scheduled access.

Vendor Management System

Your vendor network is your maintenance backbone. Build it before you need it.

Essential Vendor Categories

  • General handyman — handles 60% of routine requests
  • Plumber — licensed, 24/7 emergency available
  • Electrician — licensed, familiar with residential codes
  • HVAC technician — maintains, repairs, and installs systems
  • Locksmith — 24/7 emergency, rekeying services
  • Appliance repair — covers all major appliances
  • Pest control — quarterly preventive + emergency service
  • Landscaping — weekly/biweekly service
  • Roofing — emergency tarping + full replacement
  • Cleaning service — turnover cleans, deep cleaning

Vendor Vetting Checklist

  1. Verify license and insurance (general liability + workers' comp)
  2. Check online reviews (Google, Yelp, BBB)
  3. Request references from other PM companies
  4. Negotiate volume pricing (you bring consistent work)
  5. Agree on response times (emergencies, urgent, routine)
  6. Set up invoicing process (net-15 or net-30, digital invoices)
  7. Create a vendor agreement covering quality standards and warranty

Vendor Pricing Strategy

Negotiate volume discounts with your preferred vendors. With 100+ doors, you have leverage:

  • Ask for 10-15% below retail pricing in exchange for guaranteed volume
  • Negotiate flat-rate pricing for common jobs (unclog drain = $125, rekey locks = $75)
  • Set up a preferred vendor tier: they get first call, you give them consistent work
  • Always maintain backup vendors for each category (your primary might be unavailable)

Maintenance Cost Control

The goal is predictable, controlled maintenance spending — not eliminating it. Deferred maintenance always costs more in the long run.

Budgeting Rules of Thumb

  • Annual maintenance budget: 1-2% of property value, or $1,000-1,500 per unit
  • Reserve for capital expenditures: $200-300/unit/year minimum
  • Emergency fund: $500-1,000/unit in owner reserves

Cost Reduction Strategies

  1. Batch routine work orders — Send your handyman to 5 properties in one trip instead of 5 separate trips
  2. Use tenant self-service for minor issues — Provide video guides for garbage disposal resets, circuit breaker resets, toilet flapper replacement
  3. Preventive maintenance — A $150 HVAC service prevents a $5,000 compressor failure
  4. Negotiate vendor rates annually — Your volume grows, your rates should improve
  5. Track costs per unit and per category — Identify outlier properties and systemic issues

Tenant Communication During Maintenance

How you communicate about maintenance directly impacts tenant satisfaction and retention:

  • Acknowledge every request within 2 hours — even if you can't fix it yet, confirm you received it
  • Provide a timeline — "A plumber will be there between 10am-12pm tomorrow"
  • Follow up after completion — "Was the issue resolved to your satisfaction?"
  • Give 24-48 hour notice for non-emergency entry — required by law in most states
  • Explain owner-approved delays — if owner approval is needed for a large expense, tell the tenant you're working on it
