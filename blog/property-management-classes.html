State Licensing Classes (Start Here)

If you're new to property management, your first step is meeting your state's licensing requirements. In most states, this means completing a pre-licensing education course.

What to Expect

  • Hours: 40–150 hours depending on state (California requires 135 hours for broker)
  • Format: Online or classroom — both accepted in most states
  • Cost: $200–$800 for the course; exam fees are typically $50–$100 additional
  • Timeline: 2 weeks to 6 months depending on your pace

Top Online Licensing Schools

  • Kaplan Real Estate Education — available in most states, strong reputation, good pass rates
  • The CE Shop — modern platform, mobile-friendly, good customer support
  • Colibri Real Estate (formerly Real Estate Express) — competitive pricing, available in all 50 states
  • Mbition — lower cost option with decent content

Tip: Don't overthink which licensing school to use. They all teach the same state-mandated curriculum. Pick the one with the best price and reviews in your state, and get through it quickly. The real education happens on the job. Check your state's requirements.

Professional Certifications (Career Accelerators)

Once you're licensed and working, professional certifications are the single best investment in your PM career. They boost credibility, open doors to higher-paying positions, and demonstrate expertise to property owners.

ARM — Accredited Residential Manager (Best for Beginners)

  • Organization: IREM (Institute of Real Estate Management)
  • Requirements: Complete coursework + pass exam. No experience requirement.
  • Cost: $500–$1,000 (coursework + exam + IREM membership)
  • Time: 2–4 months part-time
  • Value: Great entry credential. Shows employers you're serious and knowledgeable.

CPM — Certified Property Manager (The Gold Standard)

  • Organization: IREM
  • Requirements: 36 months PM experience + coursework + capstone project + ethics course
  • Cost: $2,500–$5,000 total (courses + exam + membership)
  • Time: 12–18 months
  • Value: The most respected credential in property management. CPMs earn 20–30% more than non-certified peers. Required or preferred for many senior roles.

CAM — Certified Apartment Manager

  • Organization: NAA (National Apartment Association)
  • Requirements: 12 months experience + coursework + exam
  • Cost: $1,500–$3,000
  • Time: 3–6 months
  • Value: Essential if you're in multifamily/apartment management. Industry standard for on-site and regional managers.

RMP/MPM — NARPM Designations

  • Organization: NARPM (National Association of Residential Property Managers)
  • RMP (Residential Management Professional): For PM company owners/managers. Requires 100+ units, coursework, and ethical standards. $500–$1,500.
  • MPM (Master Property Manager): Advanced designation for established companies. Requires RMP + additional experience + 500+ units managed.
  • Value: Best for residential PM company owners. NARPM is the premier residential PM association — their designations carry weight with property owners.

Online Courses (Budget-Friendly)

If you want to learn before committing to a certification program, or you're on a tight budget, these platforms offer solid PM education:

Udemy

  • Multiple PM courses ranging from $15–$100 (frequent sales)
  • Self-paced, lifetime access
  • Quality varies — check ratings and reviews before buying
  • Good for: specific skills (tenant screening, fair housing, maintenance management)

Coursera

  • University-affiliated courses with certificates
  • More structured than Udemy, often with peer interaction
  • Financial aid available
  • Good for: people who want academic credentials alongside practical knowledge

LinkedIn Learning

  • PM and real estate courses included with LinkedIn Premium ($30/month)
  • Professional, polished content
  • Certificates display on your LinkedIn profile
  • Good for: job seekers who want to signal PM knowledge to recruiters

How to Choose the Right Class

Decision framework:

  1. Not yet licensed? → State licensing course (mandatory first step)
  2. New to PM, want credibility? → ARM certification ($500–$1,000)
  3. In multifamily? → CAM certification ($1,500–$3,000)
  4. Own a PM company? → RMP designation ($500–$1,500)
  5. Want the top credential? → CPM designation ($2,500–$5,000)
  6. Exploring on a budget? → Udemy course ($15–$50) + our free SOPs
  7. Want practical, not theoretical? → PM Scaling Kit ($147)

Tips for Getting the Most from PM Classes

  • Apply immediately. After each module, implement what you learned in your actual work. Theory without practice is forgettable.
  • Network. The relationships you build in certification programs are often more valuable than the content. Your classmates become referral sources, mentors, and collaborators.
  • Don't chase credentials endlessly. One strong certification (CPM or CAM) plus real experience trumps a resume full of certificates.
  • Budget for continuing education. Most designations require annual CE credits. Factor this into your cost calculation.
  • Check employer reimbursement. Many PM companies pay for employee certifications. Ask before paying out of pocket.
