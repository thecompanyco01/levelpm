Property Management Software Reviews (2026)

Honest ratings based on features, pricing, ease of use, and real PM feedback

ScaleDoors → Blog → Software Reviews

We've evaluated every major property management software platform based on five criteria that matter most to PM company owners: features, pricing, ease of use, customer support, and scalability. No affiliate bias — just honest assessments to help you pick the right tool.

💡 Bottom line: For companies scaling from 50 to 500 doors, Buildium offers the best overall value. For 500+ units, AppFolio's automation justifies the premium. For startups under 50 units, TenantCloud's free plan is unbeatable.

Our Ratings at a Glance

1. AppFolio

8.8/10

Best for: Growing companies with 200+ units who need automation

Pricing: $1.49/unit/mo (Core), $3.20/unit/mo (Plus) — minimum $298/mo

✅ Pros

  • Best-in-class mobile app
  • AI-powered maintenance triage
  • Excellent owner portal
  • Strong accounting suite
  • Built-in CRM for owner leads

❌ Cons

  • High minimum ($298/mo) prices out small PMs
  • No month-to-month contract (annual required)
  • Limited customization for complex portfolios
  • Per-unit pricing gets expensive at scale
Verdict: AppFolio is the gold standard for mid-market PM companies. If you have 200+ units and want a modern, mobile-first platform with strong automation, it's hard to beat. The minimum spend prices out smaller operators, which is intentional — AppFolio optimizes for growing companies, not startups.

2. Buildium

8.5/10

Best for: PM companies with 20–500 units who want value + flexibility

Pricing: $58/mo (Essential), $183/mo (Growth), $375/mo (Premium)

✅ Pros

  • Most flexible pricing (no per-unit minimum)
  • Excellent community association features
  • Clean, intuitive interface
  • No setup fee
  • Good learning resources

❌ Cons

  • ACH fees add up ($1.25/transaction)
  • Mobile app less polished than AppFolio
  • Reporting is adequate but not exceptional
  • Limited maintenance vendor management
Verdict: Buildium is the best all-around choice for most PM companies. The Essential plan at $58/month is a steal for up to 150 units. The biggest downside is ACH payment fees, which can add $100–300/month for larger portfolios. If you manage HOAs alongside rentals, Buildium's community association features are best-in-class.

3. Rent Manager

8.3/10

Best for: Large, complex portfolios (500+ units) needing maximum customization

Pricing: Custom quote only (estimated $1–3/unit/mo)

✅ Pros

  • Most customizable platform available
  • Handles mixed-use (residential, commercial, storage)
  • On-premise deployment option
  • Powerful reporting and accounting
  • Excellent API for integrations

❌ Cons

  • Steep learning curve
  • No public pricing (frustrating)
  • Interface feels dated compared to competitors
  • Requires more training investment
Verdict: Rent Manager is the power tool of PM software. If you need to manage 500+ units across multiple property types with complex accounting needs, it's arguably the best option. But it's overkill for most companies under 500 units, and the learning curve means you need to invest in proper training for your team.

4. TenantCloud

7.8/10

Best for: Startups and small PMs with under 75 units

Pricing: Free (up to 75 units), $15/mo (Growth), $50/mo (Business)

✅ Pros

  • Free plan covers up to 75 units
  • Modern, clean interface
  • Good tenant and owner portals
  • Built-in marketing tools
  • Easy to learn

❌ Cons

  • Limited reporting on free plan
  • Fewer integrations than competitors
  • Customer support can be slow
  • Not suitable for 200+ units
Verdict: TenantCloud is the best starter platform. If you're managing under 75 units and want to keep costs at zero while you grow, it's a no-brainer. Just plan to migrate to Buildium or AppFolio once you outgrow it.

5. DoorLoop

7.5/10

Best for: Mixed portfolios (residential + commercial) wanting simplicity

Pricing: $59/mo (Starter), $119/mo (Pro), $149/mo (Premium)

✅ Pros

  • Very easy to set up and use
  • Handles residential + commercial
  • Good onboarding experience
  • QuickBooks integration

❌ Cons

  • Relatively new (less market-tested)
  • Fewer advanced features than AppFolio/Buildium
  • Limited ecosystem of add-ons
  • Some features feel half-baked
Verdict: DoorLoop is a solid mid-tier option. It won't blow you away with features, but it's easy to use and reasonably priced. Good for PMs who want simplicity over power.

6. Propertyware

7.2/10

Best for: Single-family rental specialists with 250+ units

Pricing: $1/unit/mo (Basic), $1.50 (Plus), $2 (Premium) — min $250/mo

✅ Pros

  • Purpose-built for single-family
  • Backed by RealPage (enterprise stability)
  • Strong marketing/lead capture tools
  • Good inspection features

❌ Cons

  • High setup fees (2x monthly)
  • Interface feels corporate and clunky
  • Not ideal for multifamily
  • Customer support mixed reviews
Verdict: If you exclusively manage single-family rentals at scale, Propertyware has features tailored to your needs. But the high minimums and setup fees make it a hard sell against Buildium or AppFolio for mixed portfolios.

What Matters More Than Software

Here's the truth that software vendors won't tell you: the best PM software in the world can't fix broken operations.

We've seen PM companies using spreadsheets outperform competitors on AppFolio — because they had tight SOPs, trained teams, and systematic processes. Software amplifies what you already have. If your operations are chaotic, software just makes the chaos faster.

Before you invest $100–2,000/month in PM software, invest in your operational foundation:

  • Documented SOPs for every repeatable process (move-in, move-out, maintenance, inspections)
  • Clear team roles — who handles what, with no overlap or gaps
  • Financial systems — trust accounting, owner reporting, P&L tracking
  • Training programs — so every new hire gets up to speed in days, not months

Final Recommendations

  • Under 75 units: Start with TenantCloud (free). Invest in SOPs and processes.
  • 75–200 units: Buildium Essential ($58/mo). Best value in the market.
  • 200–500 units: AppFolio Core ($1.49/unit). Automation pays for itself.
  • 500+ units: Rent Manager (custom quote). Maximum customization.
  • Single-family only (250+): Propertyware ($1/unit). Purpose-built.

Whatever you choose, remember: software is a tool, not a strategy. The PM companies that dominate their markets have great operations first and great software second.
