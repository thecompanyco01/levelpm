1 How to Use This Checklist

This inspection checklist is designed to be completed twice for every tenancy: once at move-in and once at move-out. The move-in inspection establishes the baseline condition; the move-out inspection documents any changes. The difference between the two determines what, if anything, is deducted from the security deposit.

Best practices for inspections:

  • Always have the tenant present for move-in inspections. Both parties sign the completed form.
  • For move-out, offer the tenant the opportunity to attend. If they decline or are unavailable, note that on the form and proceed.
  • Use natural daylight when possible — it reveals condition issues that artificial lighting can hide.
  • Take photos BEFORE the tenant moves belongings in (move-in) or AFTER all belongings are removed (move-out).
  • Never rush an inspection. A thorough 45-minute inspection saves hours of deposit disputes later.
  • Rate every line item. "N/A" is acceptable when the item doesn't exist in that unit. Blank entries look like you missed something.

6 Security Deposit Deduction Guidelines

Security deposit deductions must be reasonable, documented, and comply with your state's landlord-tenant laws. Most states require an itemized statement within 14–30 days of move-out.

General Deduction Principles

  1. Normal wear and tear is NOT deductible. The property is expected to show age proportional to the tenancy length.
  2. Damage beyond normal wear IS deductible. Charge actual cost to repair/replace, minus depreciation for the item's age.
  3. Cleaning charges are deductible only if the unit was not left in "broom clean" condition (or whatever your lease specifies). Always compare to move-in photos.
  4. Pro-rate replacements. If carpet has a 10-year useful life and the tenant damaged it in year 3, they owe 70% of replacement cost — not 100%.
  5. Provide receipts or estimates. Most states require actual invoices or reasonable estimates for each deduction.

8 Move-In Welcome Packet Checklist

Deliver this packet to the tenant on move-in day (or email digitally). A great first impression reduces maintenance calls and builds trust.

  • Welcome letter — personalized with tenant name, property address, your contact information
  • Emergency contact information — office hours line, after-hours emergency number, 911 reminders
  • Maintenance request instructions — how to submit (portal preferred), what constitutes an emergency
  • Utility setup confirmation — which utilities tenant is responsible for, local provider names/numbers
  • Trash/recycling schedule — pickup days, bin locations, what's recyclable
  • HOA rules summary (if applicable) — parking rules, quiet hours, pet areas, pool rules
  • Rent payment instructions — portal login, due date, grace period, late fee policy
  • Renter's insurance requirement — minimum coverage, proof due date, recommended providers
  • Key/access device inventory — signed list of all keys, remotes, fobs issued
  • HVAC filter information — filter size, recommended change schedule, where to buy
  • Water shut-off location — with photo or diagram showing where the main shut-off valve is
  • Electrical panel location — with photo showing the panel and any labeled breakers
  • Smoke/CO detector info — locations, testing instructions, battery replacement policy
  • Move-in inspection form — signed copy for tenant, signed copy for your files
  • Local area guide (optional but impressive) — nearest grocery, pharmacy, hospital, schools

10 Turnover Coordination Workflow

The goal: minimize vacancy days. A well-coordinated turnover takes 5–7 days. A disorganized one takes 14–21 days. That's $500–$1,500+ in lost rent.

Day-by-Day Turnover Playbook

DayTaskVendor/Person
Day 0Move-out inspection completed. Work scope defined. Vendors notified.Property Manager
Day 1Trash-out (if needed). Deep cleaning crew starts. Re-key all locks.Cleaners, Locksmith
Day 1–2Paint touch-up or full repaint (as needed). Drywall repairs.Painter
Day 2–3Carpet cleaning or replacement. Hard floor cleaning/repair.Flooring vendor
Day 3–4Appliance repair/replacement. Plumbing fixes. Electrical fixes.Handyman/Specialists
Day 4–5HVAC service/filter change. Smoke detector batteries. Final repairs.HVAC tech, Handyman
Day 5–6Landscaping/exterior touch-up. Final clean (touch-up after vendors).Landscaper, Cleaners
Day 6–7Final walk-through. Photography for listing. Unit listed/marketed.Property Manager
Pro tip: Start marketing the unit 30 days before move-out with "available [date]" listings. Schedule showings during the last week of occupancy (with tenant permission and proper notice). A signed lease on Day 1 of vacancy is the goal.
