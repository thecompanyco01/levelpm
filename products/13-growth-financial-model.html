The Profitability Valley
Notice the "valley" between 150–350 doors? This is real. You're hiring ahead of revenue. Margins compress dramatically as you build the team needed for scale. The goal is to push through to 400+ doors where economies of scale kick in and margins recover to 20–25%. Many PMs stall in the valley because they don't plan for the cash flow squeeze. Plan for it.

Funding Your Growth
Most PM companies self-fund through cash flow. Avoid debt unless you're acquiring another company's management contracts. The best capital source is retained earnings from your existing portfolio. Set aside 10–15% of net profit every month into a growth fund. At 100 doors with $11K/mo net profit, that's $1,100–$1,650/month — enough to fund your next hire within 3–6 months.

Key Insight
Notice that aggressive growth produces MORE total revenue but LESS profit in the short term. The payoff comes in Year 2 and beyond when those 210 doors generate $500K+ annually and your team costs are amortized across more units. Growth is an investment. Make sure your cash reserves can fund the journey.
