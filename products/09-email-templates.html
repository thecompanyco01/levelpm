47 Email Templates

Copy-paste emails for every property management scenario. Tenant communications, owner updates, vendor coordination, leasing, and internal team — all customizable with your company details.


Email Categories

  1. Tenant Communications (15 emails)
  2. Owner Communications (12 emails)
  3. Vendor Communications (8 emails)
  4. Leasing & Marketing (7 emails)
  5. Internal Team (5 emails)
How to use: Copy any template, replace the [bracketed placeholders] with your company details, and send. Each template includes the subject line, body, and notes on when to use it.

Section 1: Tenant Communications (15 Emails)

Tenant

1. Late Rent Notice — Day 1

Subject: Friendly Reminder — Rent Due for [Property Address]
Send: Day after rent due date (typically the 2nd of the month)
Hi [Tenant Name], This is a friendly reminder that your rent payment of [$Amount] for [Property Address] was due on [Due Date]. As of today, we have not received your payment. You can submit payment through the tenant portal at [Portal URL], or contact our office at [Phone] if you need to discuss payment arrangements. Please note that a late fee of [$Late Fee Amount] will be assessed after [Grace Period Date] per your lease agreement. Thank you, [Your Name] [Company Name] [Phone]

2. Late Rent Notice — Day 5 (Formal)

Subject: PAST DUE — Rent Payment Required for [Property Address]
Send: 5 days after due date / after grace period expires
Dear [Tenant Name], Your rent payment of [$Amount] for [Property Address], Unit [Unit], is now [X] days past due. A late fee of [$Late Fee] has been applied to your account per your lease agreement (Section [X]). Your current balance due is: [$Total Including Late Fee] Please submit payment immediately through your tenant portal or contact our office to discuss a payment arrangement. If we do not receive payment or hear from you by [Date — typically 3 days from this notice], we will be required to proceed with formal collection procedures as outlined in your lease and applicable state law. We understand that circumstances arise. If you are experiencing a temporary hardship, please contact us to discuss options. Sincerely, [Your Name] [Company Name]

3. Late Rent Notice — Day 10 (Final / Pre-Legal)

Subject: URGENT: Final Notice — Past Due Rent for [Property Address]
Send: 10 days past due / before initiating formal pay-or-quit notice
Dear [Tenant Name], This is your final notice regarding the past-due rent for [Property Address], Unit [Unit]. Amount Due: [$Rent + Late Fees] Days Past Due: [X] Despite previous notices on [Date 1] and [Date 2], we have not received payment or a communication regarding payment arrangements. If full payment is not received by [Date], we will be required to serve a formal [Pay or Quit / Pay or Vacate] notice as required by [State] law. This may be the first step in the eviction process. Please contact our office immediately at [Phone] to resolve this matter. Sincerely, [Your Name] [Company Name]

4. Lease Renewal Offer

Subject: Your Lease Renewal at [Property Address] — Options Inside
Send: 60-90 days before lease expiration
Hi [Tenant Name], Your current lease at [Property Address] expires on [Expiration Date]. We've enjoyed having you as a tenant and would love to offer you the following renewal options: Option 1: 12-Month Renewal Monthly rent: [$New Rent]/month (effective [Start Date]) Lease term: [Start Date] through [End Date] Option 2: Month-to-Month Monthly rent: [$MTM Rent — typically 5-10% higher]/month Requires 30-day notice to vacate from either party Please let us know your preference by [Response Deadline — 30 days from this email]. If we don't hear from you by that date, your lease will convert to a month-to-month agreement at the month-to-month rate. To accept a renewal option, simply reply to this email or log into your tenant portal and sign the renewal agreement. Thank you for being a great tenant! Best, [Your Name] [Company Name]

5. Maintenance Request Received

Subject: Maintenance Request Received — [Issue Summary]
Send: Within 2 hours of receiving any maintenance request
Hi [Tenant Name], We've received your maintenance request regarding [Issue — e.g., "leaking kitchen faucet"] at [Property Address]. Work Order #: [WO Number] Priority: [Routine / Urgent / Emergency] Expected Timeline: [Within 24-72 hours / Within 4 hours for emergencies] Our maintenance team will [contact you to schedule access / dispatch a vendor today]. Please ensure [access instructions — e.g., "the unit is accessible" or "your pet is secured"]. If you have any questions, reply to this email or call [Phone]. Thank you, [Your Name] [Company Name]

6. Maintenance Completed Notification

Subject: Maintenance Complete — [Issue Summary]
Send: Within 24 hours of work order completion
Hi [Tenant Name], Good news — the maintenance request for [Issue] at [Property Address] has been completed. Work Order #: [WO Number] Work Performed: [Brief description of what was done] Completed By: [Vendor Name] on [Date] If the issue isn't fully resolved or if you notice anything else, please let us know immediately and we'll send the vendor back at no additional hassle to you. Best, [Your Name]

7. Move-In Welcome Email

Subject: Welcome to [Property Address] — Everything You Need to Know
Send: 3-5 days before move-in date
Welcome [Tenant Name]! We're excited to welcome you to [Property Address]. Here's everything you need for a smooth move-in: 📅 Move-In Date: [Date] 🔑 Key Pickup: [Location, time, and what to bring (photo ID)] 📋 Move-In Inspection: Scheduled for [Date/Time] — please be present to walk through the unit together Important Setup Items: • Set up utilities in your name by [Date]: [Electric: Company/Phone, Gas: Company/Phone, Water: Company/Phone] • Renter's insurance must be active by move-in — upload proof to your tenant portal • Tenant portal: [URL] — set up your account to pay rent online and submit maintenance requests Your Key Contacts: • General inquiries: [Phone] / [Email] • Maintenance requests: Submit through your tenant portal (preferred) or call [Phone] • After-hours emergencies ONLY: [Emergency Phone] WiFi Password (if applicable): [Network / Password] Trash/Recycling Day: [Day] Parking: [Assigned spot # or instructions] Welcome to the neighborhood! Let us know if you need anything. Best, [Your Name] [Company Name]

8. Move-Out Instructions

Subject: Move-Out Checklist & Instructions — [Property Address]
Send: 30 days before lease end / upon receiving notice to vacate
Hi [Tenant Name], We've received your notice to vacate [Property Address] effective [Move-Out Date]. To ensure a smooth move-out and maximum security deposit return, please complete the following: Move-Out Checklist: ☐ Remove ALL personal belongings (items left behind will incur removal charges) ☐ Clean the entire unit: kitchen appliances, bathrooms, floors, windows ☐ Patch small nail holes with spackle (leave larger holes for us) ☐ Replace any burned-out light bulbs ☐ Replace HVAC filter ☐ Professional carpet cleaning (receipt required for deposit return) ☐ Mow lawn / remove yard debris (if applicable) ☐ Return ALL keys, garage remotes, and access cards to our office by [Date/Time] Move-Out Inspection: Scheduled for [Date/Time]. We strongly recommend you attend so we can note any items together. Utilities: Keep all utilities active through [Move-Out Date]. Do not disconnect early. Security Deposit: Your deposit of [$Amount] will be returned within [X days per state law] of move-out, minus any applicable deductions for damages beyond normal wear and tear. Forwarding Address: Please reply with your forwarding address for your deposit check and any final correspondence. Questions? Call us at [Phone]. Best, [Your Name]

9. Noise / Lease Violation Warning

Subject: Lease Violation Notice — [Property Address]
Send: After verified complaint, within 24 hours
Dear [Tenant Name], We have received a [noise complaint / report of unauthorized pet / report of unauthorized occupant / other violation] regarding your unit at [Property Address] on [Date of Incident]. This is a violation of Section [X] of your lease agreement, which states: [quote relevant lease section]. Please [cease the noise after 10 PM / remove the unauthorized pet / provide documentation of the occupant for lease addition / other corrective action] within [X days] of this notice. This is a [first / second] notice. Continued violations may result in further action up to and including lease termination as permitted by your lease agreement and [State] law. If you'd like to discuss this matter, please contact our office at [Phone]. Sincerely, [Your Name] [Company Name]

10. Emergency Notification to Tenants

Subject: ⚠️ IMPORTANT: [Emergency Type] at [Property Address]
Send: Immediately during any property emergency
Dear Residents, We are writing to inform you of a [water main break / power outage / fire alarm / severe weather / gas leak / other emergency] affecting [Property Address / specific units]. What's happening: [Brief description] What we're doing: [Vendor dispatched / Utility company notified / Emergency services contacted] What you should do: [Stay in your unit / Evacuate immediately / Avoid using water / etc.] Estimated resolution: [Timeline if known, or "We will update you within X hours"] For questions, call our emergency line: [Phone]. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. We'll send another update by [Time]. [Your Name] [Company Name]

11-15. Additional Tenant Emails

11. Rent Increase Notice

Subject: Rent Adjustment Notice — [Property Address]
Send: 60+ days before effective date (check state requirements — some require 90 days)
Dear [Tenant Name], Per the terms of your lease agreement and in accordance with [State] law, this letter serves as formal notice that your monthly rent will be adjusted from [$Current Rent] to [$New Rent], effective [Date]. This adjustment reflects [market conditions / increased property taxes / insurance costs / property improvements]. The new rate remains competitive with comparable units in the area. All other terms of your lease remain unchanged. If you have questions or would like to discuss, please contact our office. Sincerely, [Your Name]

12. Tenant Portal Setup Reminder

Subject: Set Up Your Tenant Portal — Pay Rent Online & More
Send: 3 days after move-in if portal not yet activated
Hi [Tenant Name], Quick reminder to set up your tenant portal at [URL]. It takes 2 minutes and gives you: • Online rent payments (auto-pay available) • Maintenance request submission • Lease document access • Payment history Your login: [Email] / Temp password: [Password] Need help? Reply to this email or call [Phone]. [Your Name]

13. Annual Inspection Notice

Subject: Scheduled Property Inspection — [Date]
Send: 48-72 hours before inspection (check state notice requirements)
Hi [Tenant Name], Per your lease agreement and [State] landlord entry law, we will be conducting a routine property inspection at [Property Address] on: Date: [Date] Time: Between [Time Range] Purpose: Routine annual inspection — checking for maintenance needs and property condition You do not need to be present, but you are welcome to attend. Please ensure that [pets are secured / access is available]. This is a standard inspection — not a response to any complaint or issue. If you have any maintenance concerns you'd like us to address during the visit, please submit them through your portal or reply to this email. Thank you, [Your Name]

14. Lease Violation — Unauthorized Pet

Subject: Unauthorized Pet Notice — [Property Address]
Send: Upon verified discovery of unauthorized pet
Dear [Tenant Name], During a [routine inspection / maintenance visit / based on a report], an unauthorized pet was observed in your unit at [Property Address]. Per Section [X] of your lease, pets are [not permitted / require prior written approval and pet deposit]. You have [X] days to either: (1) remove the pet from the premises, or (2) submit a pet application with the required pet deposit of [$Amount] and monthly pet rent of [$Amount] for approval. Please note: This does not apply to service animals or emotional support animals with proper documentation. Contact our office to discuss: [Phone]. [Your Name]

15. Thank You — Lease Renewal Confirmation

Subject: Lease Renewal Confirmed — Thank You!
Send: Upon signing the renewal agreement
Hi [Tenant Name], Thank you for renewing your lease at [Property Address]! We're glad to have you continuing with us. New Lease Term: [Start Date] through [End Date] Monthly Rent: [$Amount] A signed copy of your renewal has been uploaded to your tenant portal. Everything else (rent payment method, maintenance procedures, contacts) stays the same. If you need anything, we're here. Enjoy your home! Best, [Your Name]

Key Highlights: • Rent Collected: [$Amount] • Total Expenses: [$Amount] • Net Disbursement: [$Amount] (deposited to your account on [Date]) • Occupancy: [100% / X of Y units occupied] Maintenance Summary: [Brief — e.g., "2 routine work orders completed: kitchen faucet replacement ($185) and HVAC filter change ($45). No open items."] Notes: [Any relevant updates — lease expirations, market conditions, recommended actions] Full details are in the attached report. Let me know if you have any questions. Best, [Your Name]

Subject: Maintenance Approval Needed — [Property Address] ([$Amount])

[Your Name]

23-27. Quick Owner Templates

23. Tax Document Ready (1099): "Your 1099 for tax year [Year] is available in your owner portal and has been mailed to [Address]. Contact your CPA with questions."

Section 3: Vendor Communications (8 Emails)

Vendor

28. Work Order Dispatch

Subject: Work Order [#WO Number] — [Property Address]
Send: When dispatching a vendor for maintenance
[Vendor Name], New work order for your attention: WO#: [Number] Property: [Address, Unit] Issue: [Description] Priority: [Routine — within 72 hrs / Urgent — within 24 hrs / Emergency — ASAP] Access: [Lockbox code / tenant will be home / key at office] Tenant Contact: [Name, Phone] Budget Limit: [$Amount — call before exceeding] Please confirm receipt and your estimated arrival. Send before/after photos and your invoice to [Email]. Thank you, [Your Name]

29. Invoice Dispute

Subject: Invoice Question — WO# [Number]
Send: When an invoice has discrepancies
Hi [Vendor Name], I'm reviewing invoice [#Invoice] for work order [#WO] at [Property Address] and have a question: [Describe discrepancy — e.g., "The invoice shows 4 hours of labor, but the work was completed in approximately 2 hours per the tenant" or "The approved budget was $300 but the invoice is $475 — what additional work was required?"] Could you provide clarification or an adjusted invoice? We want to process your payment promptly. Thanks, [Your Name]

30. Insurance Verification Request

Subject: Insurance Certificate Required — [Vendor Company]
Send: Before adding a new vendor to your approved list
Hi [Vendor Name], Before we can add [Vendor Company] to our preferred vendor list, we need the following insurance documentation: ☐ Certificate of General Liability Insurance (minimum [$1,000,000]) ☐ Workers' Compensation Insurance (or state exemption form) ☐ [Company Name] listed as Additional Insured ☐ Valid through at least [Date — 12 months out] Please have your insurance agent send the certificate directly to [Email]. Once received, we'll add you to our vendor directory and begin assigning work orders. We look forward to working with you. [Your Name]

31. Preferred Vendor Application

Subject: Preferred Vendor Application — [Company Name]
Send: When vetting new vendors
Hi [Vendor Name], Thank you for your interest in becoming a preferred vendor with [Company Name]. We manage [X] residential units and are looking for reliable [plumbing / HVAC / electrical / general maintenance] partners. To be considered, please provide: 1. Business license number 2. Insurance certificate (see requirements in separate email) 3. Three references from property management companies 4. Standard hourly rates and service call fees 5. Typical response time for routine vs. emergency calls 6. Service area coverage Our preferred vendors receive consistent work volume, prompt payment (net 30), and a long-term partnership. We pay by [check / ACH / credit card]. [Your Name]

32-35. Additional Vendor Templates

32. Vendor Performance Warning: Document subpar performance (response time, quality, communication) with specific incidents and improvement expectations.

33. Vendor Rate Negotiation: Request rate review based on volume commitment ("We're prepared to guarantee X work orders/month at $Y rate").

34. Emergency Vendor Dispatch: Urgent template with all-caps priority, immediate response required, after-hours contact info.

35. Vendor Termination: Professional notice ending the vendor relationship with reasons and final invoice instructions.

44-47. Additional Internal Templates

44. Weekly Team Meeting Agenda: Standardized agenda covering portfolio status, maintenance pipeline, leasing updates, owner concerns, and action items.

45. Maintenance Emergency Alert: Internal notification when an emergency is reported — who's responding, what's the status, owner notified?
