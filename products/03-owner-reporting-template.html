1 How to Use This Template

This template provides a complete framework for monthly owner reports. It's designed to be customized to your branding and adapted to each property's specifics. The key principles:

  • Send by the 15th of the following month. Owners expect consistency. Set a recurring deadline — the 10th is ideal for preparation, 15th for delivery.
  • Lead with the summary, not the numbers. Most owners scan — they want the headline first. Is their property performing well? Any problems? The financial details are for those who want to dig deeper.
  • Be proactive, not reactive. Don't just report what happened. Recommend what should happen next. This is how you justify your management fee.
  • Customize the narrative. Every owner is different. Some want a one-page overview. Others want every line item. Know your audience. The narrative sections below give you templates for both.
  • Always include context. "$1,200 in maintenance" means nothing without context. "$1,200 in maintenance — a water heater replacement that extends the unit's hot water system by 10+ years" tells a story.

3 Executive Summary

The executive summary is the most important section. Many owners will only read this. It should be 3–5 sentences that cover: financial performance, occupancy status, any significant events, and your top recommendation.

Template — Strong Performance:
"Your property at [Address] performed well in [Month]. Net operating income was $[X], [up/down X%] from last month. All [X] units are occupied with rent collected in full by the 5th. We completed [X] maintenance items totaling $[X] — all routine. Lease renewal for Unit [X] was executed at $[X]/month, a [X]% increase effective [date]. No significant issues or recommendations at this time."
Template — Vacancy Situation:
"Your property at [Address] had a mixed month. Net operating income was $[X], impacted by the vacancy in Unit [X] since [date]. We've had [X] showings and [X] applications to date. Market rent for comparable units is $[X]-$[X]/month; we're listed at $[X]. We recommend [adjusting price / continuing at current price / offering a move-in concession]. We anticipate the unit will be leased by [date]. All occupied units are performing well with full rent collection."
Template — Major Repair:
"Your property at [Address] required a significant repair this month. The [item — e.g., HVAC system in Unit 3] failed on [date] and was replaced at a cost of $[X]. We obtained [X] competitive bids and selected [Vendor] at the best value. This was an emergency repair necessary to maintain habitability. The new unit carries a [X]-year warranty. Outside of this repair, the property performed normally with $[X] in net operating income and full occupancy across all other units."

Maintenance Cost Context

The industry benchmark for maintenance costs is $800–$1,200 per unit per year for well-maintained properties (or approximately $67–$100/unit/month). Properties over 20 years old typically run 20–30% higher. Use this to give owners context:

Template: "Total maintenance expenditure for [Month] was $[X] across [X] work orders. This represents $[X] per unit, which is [below/at/above] the industry benchmark of $67-100/unit/month. [If above:] The elevated cost this month was driven by [specific item], which is a one-time expense. Trailing 12-month average remains at $[X]/unit/month."

10 Recommendations

This section is where you earn your management fee. Proactive, specific recommendations show owners you're thinking about their investment — not just collecting rent.

Recommendation Categories

🔧 Maintenance / Capital Improvements

Template: "We recommend [specific action] at an estimated cost of $[X]. This is needed because [reason / what we observed during inspection / age of equipment]. If deferred, the likely consequence is [higher repair cost / tenant complaint / code violation / property damage]. We recommend scheduling this for [timeframe]. We have obtained [X] estimates — see attached."

💰 Revenue Optimization

Template: "Based on current market conditions, we recommend [raising rent by $X / implementing a pet fee / adding utility reimbursement]. Comparable properties in the area are charging $[X] for similar units. This change would increase annual revenue by approximately $[X]. We recommend implementing at [next renewal / immediately for new leases]."

📋 Lease / Tenant

Template: "Tenant in Unit [X] has [specific situation — e.g., multiple late payments, lease violation, requested lease modification]. We recommend [specific action]. This is consistent with our lease terms and local regulations. We'll keep you updated on next steps."

🏠 Property Improvement ROI

Template: "We've identified an opportunity to increase rental value through [specific improvement — e.g., adding washer/dryer hookups, updating kitchen counters, installing smart locks]. Estimated cost: $[X]. Expected rent increase: $[X]/month. This investment would pay for itself in [X] months and increase property value by approximately $[X]. Let us know if you'd like us to obtain detailed estimates."

11 Additional Sample Narratives

Scenario: Rent Increase Implemented

"The rent increase for Unit [X] took effect on [date], moving from $[old] to $[new] per month — a [X]% increase. The tenant signed the renewal without negotiation. This brings annual gross income for this unit to $[X], an increase of $[X]/year. The new rate is in line with market comparables and positions us well for continued modest annual increases."

Scenario: Eviction in Progress

"Eviction proceedings continue for Unit [X]. Timeline to date: [3-day notice served on X, unlawful detainer filed on X, court date scheduled for X]. Outstanding balance is $[X]. Our attorney estimates resolution by [date]. We are actively marketing the unit for availability upon possession. Estimated turnover and re-leasing cost: $[X]. Legal fees to date: $[X]. We'll update you after the court hearing."

Scenario: Insurance Claim

"A [type of incident — e.g., water damage from upstairs unit pipe burst] occurred on [date]. We filed a claim with [insurance company] on [date], claim #[X]. The adjuster visited on [date] and assessed damage at approximately $[X]. Temporary repairs were completed immediately to prevent further damage ($[X]). Full restoration is pending insurance approval, estimated at [X] weeks. The affected tenant [was/was not] displaced — [details of any tenant accommodation if applicable]."

Scenario: Market Downturn / Extended Vacancy

"Unit [X] has been vacant for [X] days, exceeding our typical [X]-day average. Here's what we've done: listed on [X platforms], conducted [X] showings, received [X] applications ([X] denied due to screening criteria). The current rental market is [description — e.g., softening due to seasonal slowdown, new supply coming online in the area]. We've reduced the asking rent from $[X] to $[X] as of [date]. We're also offering [concession — e.g., $200 off first month]. We project leasing within [X] additional days at the adjusted rate."
