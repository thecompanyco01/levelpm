1. Onboarding Philosophy & Goals

New hire onboarding is your highest-leverage investment. A structured 30-day program achieves three goals:

  • Productivity: A well-onboarded employee reaches full productivity in 4 weeks. Without structure, it takes 8-12 weeks — costing you 1-2 months of salary in lost output.
  • Retention: Employees who experience structured onboarding are 58% more likely to stay past 3 years (SHRM research). Every PM hire you lose costs $4,000-8,000 in recruiting and training costs.
  • Consistency: Standardized onboarding ensures every employee learns the same processes, the same standards, and the same culture — regardless of who trains them.
Key Principle: Record every training session on video. When this employee leaves (they all do eventually), the recordings become the training program for their replacement. Build the system, not the dependency.

2. Pre-Arrival Checklist

Complete before the new employee's first day:

  • PM software account created with appropriate role-based permissions
  • Email account set up and tested
  • Phone system/extension configured
  • Desk/workspace prepared with supplies
  • All SOPs printed or shared digitally (maintenance triage, move-in/move-out, screening, etc.)
  • Welcome packet assembled: company handbook, org chart, benefits info, parking/access cards
  • IT access: computer, passwords, Wi-Fi, printer, building access
  • Training schedule printed for Week 1 (share with new hire before Day 1)
  • Mentor/buddy assigned (experienced team member for questions)
  • Introduce to team via email: name, role, start date, fun fact
  • First-week meetings pre-scheduled on new hire's calendar
  • Background check and I-9 verification completed

3. Week 1: Foundation & Culture

Week 1 — Company Culture, Software Training, Shadowing

Day 1 — Welcome & Orientation

  • 9:00-9:30 — Welcome meeting with manager: company mission, culture, expectations
  • 9:30-10:30 — Office tour, team introductions, workspace setup
  • 10:30-12:00 — HR paperwork: W-4, I-9, benefits enrollment, handbook review
  • 12:00-1:00 — Team lunch (don't let them eat alone on Day 1)
  • 1:00-3:00 — Company overview: portfolio size, markets, property types, client base
  • 3:00-4:30 — PM software login, basic navigation, dashboard orientation
  • 4:30-5:00 — End-of-day debrief with manager: questions, first impressions

Day 2 — Software Deep-Dive

  • 9:00-12:00 — PM software training: property records, tenant records, lease management
  • 1:00-3:00 — PM software training: work orders, maintenance module, vendor records
  • 3:00-4:30 — PM software training: financial module overview (rent posting, AP, reports)
  • 4:30-5:00 — Practice exercises: navigate to 5 specific records, enter a test work order

Day 3 — SOP Review

  • 9:00-10:30 — Review Maintenance Triage SOP (walk through with real examples)
  • 10:30-12:00 — Review Move-In/Move-Out Checklist (walk through with recent turnover)
  • 1:00-2:30 — Review Tenant Screening SOP and Fair Housing compliance
  • 2:30-4:00 — Review Owner Communication standards and reporting templates
  • 4:00-5:00 — Quiz: 10 scenario-based questions to verify SOP understanding

Day 4 — Shadow Day (AM: Field / PM: Office)

  • 9:00-12:00 — Shadow experienced PM on property visits: inspection, tenant meeting, vendor check
  • 1:00-3:00 — Shadow office operations: watch tenant calls, maintenance dispatch, owner communication
  • 3:00-5:00 — Debrief: what did you observe? Questions? Compare to SOPs.

Day 5 — Week 1 Review

  • 9:00-10:00 — Week 1 knowledge check with manager (verbal + written quiz)
  • 10:00-12:00 — Practice: process 3 mock maintenance requests using the SOP
  • 1:00-3:00 — Practice: complete a mock move-in inspection using the checklist
  • 3:00-4:00 — Feedback session: what's clear, what's confusing, what needs more training
  • 4:00-5:00 — Set goals for Week 2, preview the Week 2 schedule

4. Week 2: Process Training

Week 2 — Hands-On Process Training (Supervised)

Days 6-7 — Maintenance & Tenant Communication

  • Handle real maintenance requests with supervisor approval at each step
  • Answer tenant calls/emails — supervisor reviews before sending responses
  • Practice vendor dispatch: identify correct vendor, communicate scope, schedule
  • Document everything in PM software

Days 8-9 — Leasing & Rent Collection

  • Attend 2-3 property showings with experienced leasing agent
  • Process a real lease application using the Screening SOP (supervised)
  • Review rent ledger, identify delinquent accounts, draft collection notices
  • Practice late rent follow-up calls using scripts

Day 10 — Week 2 Review

  • Knowledge check: 15 scenario-based questions covering all processes
  • Review work completed during the week: quality, accuracy, timeliness
  • Identify areas needing additional training before Week 3 independence
  • Feedback session and Week 3 goal-setting

5. Week 3: Supervised Independent Work

Week 3 — Independent Work with Daily Check-Ins

The employee handles their assigned tasks independently. Supervisor reviews work at end of each day and provides feedback.

Daily Structure

  • 9:00 AM — 15-minute morning huddle: review today's priorities, questions from yesterday
  • 9:15-12:00 — Independent work on assigned tasks
  • 1:00-4:30 — Independent work with supervisor available for questions
  • 4:30-5:00 — End-of-day review: what was completed, what needs follow-up, quality check

Supervisor Responsibilities

  • Review all outgoing tenant communications before sending (days 11-13)
  • Approve all maintenance dispatches over $200 (days 11-15)
  • Spot-check 3-5 data entry items daily for accuracy
  • By Day 15, begin reducing review to sampling (20-30% of work)

6. Week 4: Assessment & Transition to Full Independence

Week 4 — Final Assessment & Full Transition

Days 16-19 — Fully Independent

  • Employee handles all assigned tasks independently
  • Supervisor available for questions but does not proactively review
  • Employee manages their own daily schedule and priorities
  • Weekly check-in replaces daily review

Day 20 — 30-Day Assessment

  • Formal 30-day performance review (use template — Section 9)
  • Competency assessment across all trained areas
  • Set 60-day goals and identify growth areas
  • Celebrate: acknowledge progress and welcome to the team officially

8. Training Completion Checklist

Employee must demonstrate competency in each area before signing off:

Core Competencies (All Roles)

  • PM software: can navigate, enter data, generate reports independently
  • Fair Housing: passed written quiz with 90%+ score
  • Maintenance Triage SOP: correctly categorized 10 sample requests
  • Communication standards: 3 outgoing messages reviewed and approved by supervisor
  • Emergency procedures: can articulate emergency response protocol
  • Company culture: understands mission, values, and service standards

Role-Specific Competencies

Property Manager:

  • Completed a property inspection independently (reviewed by supervisor)
  • Prepared an owner monthly report (reviewed by supervisor)
  • Handled 10+ maintenance requests through completion
  • Conducted 3+ tenant interactions (call or in-person) independently

Leasing Agent:

  • Processed 5+ applications using Screening SOP
  • Conducted 5+ property showings (at least 2 independently)
  • Created 3+ vacancy listings with professional photos and descriptions
  • Executed a lease from application to move-in (supervised)

Maintenance Coordinator:

  • Dispatched 20+ work orders correctly (vendor, scope, timeline)
  • Followed up and closed 15+ work orders through completion
  • Processed 10+ vendor invoices with proper coding
  • Handled 3+ after-hours emergency dispatches (or simulated scenarios)

60-Day Review Focus

  • Is the employee independently productive without daily oversight?
  • Are quality metrics (response time, accuracy, tenant satisfaction) at target?
  • Have the Week 4 improvement areas been addressed?
  • Set 90-day goals: specific KPI targets based on role

90-Day Review Focus

  • Full performance evaluation against role KPIs
  • Decision point: confirm permanent employment or extend probation
  • Career development conversation: where do they want to grow?
  • Set annual goals and development plan
  • Compensation review if applicable

10. Common Onboarding Mistakes

Mistake 1: Information overload on Day 1. Don't dump 8 hours of training on Day 1. The employee won't retain it. Spread foundational training across the full first week with hands-on practice between sessions.
Mistake 2: No structured schedule. "Shadow Sarah for a week" is not onboarding. It's hoping Sarah teaches the right things. Create a specific day-by-day schedule with clear learning objectives.
Mistake 3: Skipping Fair Housing training. A new leasing agent who makes a discriminatory statement during a showing exposes your company to a lawsuit. Fair Housing training is Day 1, non-negotiable, with a written assessment.
Mistake 4: No buddy/mentor. New employees have dozens of small questions they won't ask their boss. Assign a peer mentor they can approach comfortably. This reduces anxiety and accelerates learning.
Mistake 5: Not recording training sessions. When this employee leaves, you'll wish you had those recordings. Record every training session on Loom or Zoom — they become your training library.
