1. Purpose & Vendor Strategy

Your vendor network is the backbone of your maintenance operation. Bad vendors create bad tenant experiences, owner complaints, and liability exposure. This SOP standardizes how you find, vet, manage, and evaluate every vendor who touches your properties.

Target vendor count by category: Maintain a minimum of 2 preferred vendors per major category (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, general) and 1 per minor category (locksmith, pest control, appliance). Never be dependent on a single vendor in any category.

2. Vendor Vetting Checklist

Complete this checklist before adding any new vendor to your approved list:

  • Business license: Valid state/local business license verified (record number: ________)
  • Trade license: Applicable trade license for specialty work (plumbing, electrical, HVAC) — number: ________
  • General liability insurance: Minimum $1,000,000 per occurrence (certificate on file)
  • Workers' compensation: Active policy (or state exemption letter for sole proprietors)
  • Your company listed as Additional Insured on their GL policy
  • Insurance expiration date recorded and renewal reminder set
  • References checked: Minimum 3 references from other PM companies — all positive
  • Rate schedule obtained: Hourly rates, service call fees, after-hours rates documented
  • Response time commitment: Written commitment to routine (48hr) and emergency (2hr) response
  • Payment terms agreed: Net 30 standard, invoicing format, required documentation
  • W-9 collected for 1099 reporting
  • Background check: For vendors with property access (optional but recommended)
  • Preferred Vendor Agreement signed (Section 4)

4. Insurance Requirements

Vendor shall maintain the insurance coverages specified in Section 3 of this SOP for the duration of this agreement. Vendor shall provide updated certificates of insurance annually and upon any policy change. Company must be listed as Additional Insured.

5. Work Standards

  • All work performed to applicable building codes and industry standards
  • Before/after photos required for all work orders
  • Worksite left clean — no debris, tools, or materials left behind
  • Professional appearance and conduct when interacting with tenants
  • Written scope approval required before exceeding authorized budget

6. Invoicing & Payment

  • Itemized invoices required within 5 business days of work completion
  • Invoice must include: work order number, property address, description of work, labor hours, materials with receipts, before/after photos
  • Payment terms: Net 30 from invoice receipt
  • Company reserves the right to dispute charges not pre-approved or not matching agreed rates

7. Term & Termination

This agreement is effective for 12 months and auto-renews annually. Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice. Company may terminate immediately for insurance lapse, unsafe work practices, or repeated performance failures.

7. Annual Vendor Review Process

Q1: Insurance Audit

  • Verify all vendor insurance certificates are current (not expired)
  • Confirm your company is still listed as Additional Insured
  • Request updated certificates from any vendors with upcoming expirations
  • Remove any vendor whose insurance has lapsed and not been renewed

Q2: Rate Review

  • Compare vendor rates against market (get 2-3 competitor quotes per category)
  • Negotiate rate adjustments with vendors whose rates exceed market by 15%+
  • Consider volume discounts for top-utilized vendors
  • Update rate sheets in PM software

Q3: Performance Review

  • Complete the Performance Scorecard for every active preferred vendor
  • Meet with vendors scoring below 3.5 to discuss improvement areas
  • Identify gaps — any categories where you need additional vendors?
  • Begin vetting replacement vendors for underperformers

Q4: Vendor Network Planning

  • Review work order volume by category — anticipate needs for next year
  • Renew or update Preferred Vendor Agreements
  • Update emergency vendor directory with current contacts
  • Holiday/winter prep: confirm HVAC and plumbing vendors' emergency availability

10. Common Vendor Management Mistakes

Mistake 1: Using uninsured vendors "just this once." One uninsured plumber causes a flood that damages a $300K property. Your company is liable. There is no "just this once" with insurance.
Mistake 2: Single vendor dependency. Your only plumber goes on vacation during a pipe burst. Always maintain 2 preferred vendors per major category.
Mistake 3: No written rate agreement. "He usually charges about $75/hour" becomes "$150/hour for this job" very quickly. Get rates in writing before the first work order.
Mistake 4: Not tracking performance. You keep using a vendor who takes 5 days for routine work because "they're cheap." Meanwhile, 3 tenants left negative reviews. Track response time and quality — cheap + slow = expensive.
Mistake 5: Paying before verifying. Always verify work completion before paying invoices. Random spot-checks (even 10% of work orders) keep vendors honest and quality high.
