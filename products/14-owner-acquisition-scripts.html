1. Cold Call Scripts

Script A: Initial Cold Call to Property Owner

YOU: "Hi, is this [Owner Name]? My name is [Your Name] with [Company Name]. I manage residential rental properties in [Market Area]. I'm reaching out because I noticed you own [property/properties] on [Street Name] — I wanted to introduce myself in case you're ever looking for management help, or even just a second opinion on your rental strategy. Do you currently self-manage, or do you have a management company?" [IF SELF-MANAGING]: "That's great — most of our best clients started out self-managing. A lot of owners I talk to reach a point where they want their time back but aren't sure if professional management is worth the cost. Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call where I can share what we do and you can decide if it's worth exploring?" [IF ALREADY HAS A PM]: "How's that working out for you? [Listen.] That's helpful to know. If you ever consider a change or want a second opinion, I'd love to be a resource. Can I send you my info so you have it on file?" [IF NOT INTERESTED]: "Totally understand. Would it be okay if I sent you a quick email with my contact info? That way if anything changes — a bad tenant, a maintenance nightmare — you'll know who to call."

Script B: Warm Call (Referral)

YOU: "Hi [Owner Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company Name]. [Referrer Name] suggested I reach out — they mentioned you have some rental properties in [Area] and might benefit from a conversation about professional management. We currently manage [X] doors in the area and [Referrer] has been kind enough to put us in touch. Is now a good time for a quick 2-minute introduction, or would another time be better?"

2. Objection Handling Playbook

Objection: "Your fees are too high"

Response: "I understand — fees matter. Let me ask: what's a vacancy costing you per month? [Wait for answer.] For most owners, one month of vacancy costs more than a full year of our management fees. Our average days-to-fill is [X days], and our tenant retention rate is [X%]. The math usually works in your favor — but I'm happy to run the numbers for your specific properties so you can see for yourself."

Objection: "I already have a property manager"

Response: "That's great — it means you already understand the value of professional management. I'm not asking you to switch today. But I find that owners benefit from having a backup option and knowing what else is available. Would it be helpful if I sent you a comparison of what we include so you can make sure you're getting the best value?"

Objection: "I can manage it myself"

Response: "A lot of our clients said the same thing before they reached out. The ones who come to us usually hit one of three tipping points: a terrible tenant experience, a maintenance nightmare at 2 AM, or just wanting their weekends back. I'm curious — how many hours a month do you spend managing? [Listen.] At your billing rate, that time has a cost too."

Objection: "I only have one property"

Response: "One property is where most of our clients start! Managing one property well takes the same processes as managing ten — screening, maintenance, rent collection, legal compliance. The difference is whether those processes are documented and professional or ad hoc. We manage single-family homes all the way up to portfolios. Would you like to see how we'd approach yours?"

Objection: "Let me think about it"

Response: "Absolutely — this is an important decision. To help you think it through, can I send you a custom analysis for your property? It'll show your estimated net income under management vs. self-managing, including the value of your time. No pressure — just data to help you decide. What's the best email?"

5. Referral Partnership Scripts

Real Estate Agent Referral Script

YOU: "Hey [Agent Name], I know you work with a lot of investors in [Area]. I wanted to propose a referral partnership. When your investor clients buy a property and need management, send them our way. We'll treat them like gold — our owner retention rate is [X%], so they won't come back complaining about their PM. In return, when our owners want to buy or sell, we'll send them to you. We currently manage [X] doors, so there's real volume there. I can also be a resource for your listings — I can provide rental estimates to help your investor clients underwrite deals. Useful for you, free for me to provide, and it naturally leads to management conversations. Sound like something worth exploring?"

CPA / Attorney Referral Script

YOU: "Hi [CPA/Attorney Name], I'm [Name] with [Company]. We manage [X] residential rentals in [Market]. I know your clients who own rental properties often ask about management — tenants, maintenance, the operational headaches. I'd love to be your go-to recommendation when that comes up. What we can offer your clients: professional management with transparent reporting, proper trust accounting, and clean 1099 documentation that makes your job easier at tax time. In return, when our owners need [tax advice / legal counsel], you'd be our first recommendation. Could I take you to coffee to discuss?"

6. Discovery Call Framework

When a prospect agrees to a call, use this framework to qualify them and identify their pain points:

Phase 1: Rapport & Qualification (3 min)

  • "Tell me about your rental portfolio — how many properties, what type, where?"
  • "How long have you owned them?"
  • "Are you self-managing or using a PM company currently?"

Phase 2: Pain Point Discovery (5-7 min)

  • "What's working well with your current setup?"
  • "What's your biggest frustration with managing your properties?" (LISTEN — this is the key question)
  • "How many hours a month do you spend on property management?"
  • "Have you had any bad experiences with tenants or maintenance?"
  • "What would change in your life if you didn't have to manage these properties?"

Phase 3: Present Your Solution (3-5 min)

  • Connect your services directly to THEIR pain points (not a generic pitch)
  • "You mentioned [pain point] — here's specifically how we handle that..."
  • Share 1-2 relevant examples from your portfolio
  • Mention your key differentiator once, naturally

Phase 4: Next Steps (2 min)

  • "Based on what you've shared, I'd love to put together a custom proposal for your properties."
  • "I'll have it to you by [Date — within 48 hours]. Can we schedule a 15-minute call on [Date] to walk through it together?"
  • Confirm email address and any additional properties to include
