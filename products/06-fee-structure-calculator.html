1. Management Fee Calculator

Your management fee is the backbone of your revenue. Use this calculator to compare percentage-based vs. flat-fee models and find the sweet spot for your market.

Monthly Management Fee Estimator

Percentage-Based Model

Typical range: 6%–12% of collected rent

Best for: Mixed portfolios with varying rents

Pros: Revenue scales with rent increases; easy to explain; industry standard

Cons: Revenue drops with vacancies; lower-rent properties generate less

Market sweet spots:

  • SFR: 8%–10%
  • Multi-family (small): 6%–8%
  • Multi-family (50+ units): 4%–6%
  • Luxury / high-rent: 6%–8%

Flat Fee Model

Typical range: $89–$199 per door/month

Best for: Higher-rent markets; scaling quickly

Pros: Predictable revenue; easier budgeting; looks cheaper on high-rent units

Cons: Harder to justify on low-rent properties; doesn't auto-increase

Recommended tiers:

  • Basic (tenant-only): $89–$109
  • Standard (full service): $129–$159
  • Premium (full + inspections): $169–$199
💡 Pro Tip — Hybrid Model: Charge a flat fee of $99/door + 2% of collected rent. This gives you a guaranteed base while still benefiting from higher-rent properties. Example: On a $1,500/month rental, you earn $99 + $30 = $129/month (effectively 8.6%).

8. Competitive Analysis Framework

Before setting your fees, you need to know what your market looks like. Use this framework to research and position your pricing.

Competitive Research Checklist

Step 1: Identify 5–8 competitors in your market

  • Google "[your city] property management fees"
  • Check Yelp, BBB, Google Business profiles
  • Look at NARPM and local REIA member directories
  • Ask investor networking groups what they're paying

Step 2: Collect their fee data

  • Visit their websites — many list fees publicly
  • Call as a "prospective client" and request a fee schedule
  • Ask existing clients what they were charged elsewhere
  • Check if they charge flat fee or percentage

Positioning Strategies

Premium Positioning

Price at 10%–20% above market average.

Justify with: detailed reporting, guaranteed response times, tenant retention programs, owner portal, quarterly property inspections included.

Best for: Established companies with strong reviews and proven track records.

Value Positioning

Price at market average with more included services.

Win on value, not price. Include inspections, lease renewals, or basic maintenance coordination that others charge extra for.

Best for: Growing companies looking to build a portfolio quickly.

9. Fee Increase Justification Scripts

Raising fees is the fastest way to increase profitability — but it's also the scariest conversation. Use these proven scripts to make it painless.

📧 Script 1: Annual Fee Increase Email (3%–5% increase)

Subject: Your Management Fee Update — Effective [Date]

"Dear [Owner Name],

Thank you for your continued partnership. We're committed to providing exceptional service for your property at [Address].

Effective [Date], our monthly management fee will increase from [Current Fee] to [New Fee], reflecting a [X]% adjustment. This increase allows us to continue investing in the technology, staff training, and systems that keep your property performing well — including [specific improvement: new owner portal / faster maintenance response / quarterly inspections].

For context, this is our first fee adjustment in [X] months, and the increase aligns with the current rate of inflation and rising operational costs in our market.

We truly value our relationship and are proud that your property has [specific result: maintained 95%+ occupancy / seen rent increase of $X / had zero evictions under our management].

If you'd like to discuss this, I'm always available. Thank you for trusting us with your investment.

Best regards, [Your Name]"

📞 Script 2: Phone Call for Larger Increases (5%–15%)

"Hi [Owner], thanks for taking my call. I wanted to discuss our management agreement for [Address].

Over the past [time period], we've made significant investments to improve our service — [list 2-3 specific improvements]. Our costs for insurance, software, and staffing have also increased by [X]%.

To continue delivering the level of service you expect, we'll be adjusting our fee from [Current] to [New], effective [Date]. I want to be transparent: this brings us in line with the current market rate for the quality of management we provide.

I want to keep earning your business. What questions do you have?"

📧 Script 3: Fee Restructure for Underpriced Legacy Clients

Subject: Important Update to Our Service Agreement

"Dear [Owner Name],

As we continue to grow and improve our services, we periodically review our fee structures to ensure they reflect the full scope of service we provide.

When we originally began managing your property, we offered an introductory rate of [Current Fee]. Since then, we've significantly expanded our services to include [list additions: online portals, 24/7 maintenance line, quarterly inspections, etc.].

Effective [Date — give 60+ days notice], your management fee will be updated to our current standard rate of [New Fee]. This rate includes [list everything included].

We believe this reflects the true value of our service and remains competitive in the [City] market, where similar full-service management ranges from [range].

Your property has been a pleasure to manage, and we look forward to many more years of partnership.

Regards, [Your Name]"

💡 Fee Increase Best Practices:
• Give 60–90 days written notice (check your PMA for required notice period)
• Never increase more than 15% at once — stagger large increases over 2 years
• Always pair the increase with a tangible improvement or result
• Offer a "loyalty rate" that's still higher than current but below your posted rate
• Accept that 5%–10% of owners may leave — that's OK. The revenue increase from the remaining 90% more than compensates.
