2. Metric Deep-Dives

1. Occupancy Rate

What it measures: The percentage of your portfolio that is occupied and generating rent.

Occupancy Rate = (Occupied Units ÷ Total Units) × 100

Example: 145 occupied units ÷ 150 total units = 96.7% occupancy

StatusThresholdAction
🟢 Green≥ 95%Healthy. Maintain current leasing practices.
🟡 Yellow90-94%Attention needed. Review marketing and pricing. Are you priced above market?
🔴 Red< 90%Critical. Immediate pricing analysis, increased marketing spend, assess turnover causes.

2. Days-to-Lease

What it measures: Average number of days from a unit becoming vacant to a new lease being signed.

Days-to-Lease = Average(Move-Out Date → New Lease Signed Date) across all vacancies in period

Example: 5 vacancies this quarter with fill times of 18, 22, 14, 28, 15 days = 19.4 average DTL

StatusThresholdAction
🟢 Green≤ 21 daysExcellent leasing performance.
🟡 Yellow22-35 daysReview listing quality, photos, pricing. Are showings converting?
🔴 Red> 35 daysMajor issue. Pricing too high, poor listings, or turnover condition problems.

3. Cost-per-Door

What it measures: Your total operating cost to manage one door, excluding direct property expenses.

Cost-per-Door = Total Company Operating Expenses ÷ Total Doors Managed

Example: $45,000 monthly operating costs ÷ 200 doors = $225/door/month

Include: payroll, software, office, marketing, insurance, vehicle costs. Exclude: owner-paid expenses, maintenance vendor costs.

StatusThresholdAction
🟢 Green≤ $200/doorEfficient operation. Scalable.
🟡 Yellow$200-300/doorReview staffing ratios and overhead. Where can you cut?
🔴 Red> $300/doorUnsustainable. Either grow door count or cut expenses aggressively.

4. Owner Retention Rate

Owner Retention = (Owners at End of Year - New Owners Added) ÷ Owners at Start of Year × 100

Example: Started with 80 owners, ended with 90, added 15 new = (90-15)÷80 = 93.75% retention

🟢 ≥ 90%🟡 80-89%🔴 < 80%

If below 90%: Conduct exit interviews with every departing owner. Common causes: poor communication, hidden fees, maintenance quality, responsiveness.

5. Tenant Retention Rate

Tenant Retention = Lease Renewals ÷ Leases Eligible for Renewal × 100

Example: 40 leases expired this year, 30 renewed = 75% retention

🟢 ≥ 65%🟡 50-64%🔴 < 50%

Each turnover costs $1,500-3,000+ (vacancy loss + turnover costs + leasing fees). A 10% improvement in retention at 200 doors saves $30,000-60,000/year.

Avg Response Time = Average(Time Request Received → First Response/Action) across all work orders in period

7. Rent Collection Rate

Collection Rate = Total Rent Collected ÷ Total Rent Charged × 100

Example: $250,000 charged, $247,500 collected = 99.0%

🟢 ≥ 98%🟡 95-97%🔴 < 95%

8. Delinquency Rate

Delinquency Rate = Units with Past-Due Rent ÷ Total Occupied Units × 100
🟢 ≤ 3%🟡 3-8%🔴 > 8%

9. Revenue per Door

Revenue/Door = Total Management Revenue ÷ Total Doors

Includes: management fees, leasing fees, maintenance markup, admin fees. This is YOUR revenue, not rent collected.

🟢 ≥ $200/door/mo🟡 $150-199🔴 < $150

10. Profit Margin per Door

Profit/Door = (Revenue/Door - Cost/Door) = Net Profit per Door per Month

Example: $210 revenue/door - $195 cost/door = $15/door/month (7.1% margin)

🟢 ≥ 20% margin🟡 10-19%🔴 < 10%

11. Work Order Completion Rate

Completion Rate = Work Orders Completed Within SLA ÷ Total Work Orders × 100
🟢 ≥ 90%🟡 80-89%🔴 < 80%

12. Owner Satisfaction Score

Send quarterly survey (1-10 NPS-style): "How likely are you to recommend us to another property owner?"
🟢 ≥ 8.5 avg🟡 7.0-8.4🔴 < 7.0

Any owner scoring ≤ 6 gets a personal call from the PM within 48 hours to address concerns. This is your early warning system for churn.

6. Weekly Review Framework

A 30-minute weekly review keeps you on top of your KPIs before they become problems. Recommended: Monday morning.

Weekly Review Agenda (30 min)

  1. Occupancy snapshot (2 min) — Any new vacancies? Leasing pipeline status?
  2. Collection check (3 min) — Who hasn't paid? Are follow-ups happening? Escalation needed?
  3. Maintenance pipeline (5 min) — Open work orders count, aging items, any owner approval pending?
  4. Leasing pipeline (5 min) — Active showings, applications in screening, upcoming move-ins?
  5. Owner concerns (5 min) — Any owner complaints or requests from last week? Status?
  6. One red metric (5 min) — Pick the worst-performing metric. What's the plan to fix it?
  7. Wins (2 min) — What went well? Celebrate something.
  8. Action items (3 min) — 3 specific actions for the week, assigned with deadlines
The #1 rule of KPI tracking: Don't just collect data — ACT on it. A dashboard is worthless if you review it monthly and shrug. Every red metric needs a plan. Every yellow metric needs a watchlist. Every green metric needs protection. Measure → Analyze → Act → Repeat.
